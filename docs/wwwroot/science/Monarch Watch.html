Monarch Watch

 

Lesson plan for improving internet, telecommunications, and researching skills, as well

 

as, learning about migration and Monarch butterflies.

 

I. Research:

a) Research Encarta for information on Monarch butterflies:

 

1. find the life cycle of the Monarch butterfly.

 

2. draw or print a picture of a Monarch butterfly (describe in words also).

 

3. explain how the Monarch and the Viceroy butterflies are Mullerian

mimics.

 

4. relate # 3 to the Milkweed plant.

b) Research the Internet for information on Monarch butterflies:

 

1. describe Monarch migration.

 

2. relate the Monarch's life cycle to the butterfly migration.

 

3. on the provided map, draw the migration routes.

 

4. find how the Mexicans are helping in the migration.

 

5. go to Journey North and find a pattern for a Monarch

(http://www.learner.org).

 

6. write the URL's for 3 Monarch internet sites.

 

II. Make a Monarch butterfly from the pattern (explain how your butterfly will be

strong yet convey the fragile nature of a butterfly).

 

III. Set up an e-mail address for you to recieve mail from your butterfly as it travels to

Mexico. (Mrs. Haag and I will assist you in this)

 

IV. Every Friday, return to Journey North and read the challenge question posted on the

site. After reading and thinking about the question, you and your lab partner come up

with an answer to the question and e-mail it to the site provided in Journey North.

Monday of each week look to see if your answer was correct, print your answer,

making corrections if necessary, and hand it in.

 

V. From the Journey North site, plot your butterfly's migration progress on the US map

posted in the room.

 

VI. As the year continues we will be doing the following:

a) exchanging e-mail with students from other schools along the Monarch's

 

migration route.

b) setting up a bulletin board to post our Monarch information as it migrates.

c) exchange e-mail with the students in Mexico who are wintering over your

 

butterflies.

d) plotting your Monarch's return north in the spring.

f) participating in the spring watch for the return of the Monarchs to ND and

 

posting that information via e-mail to Journey North.

g) participating in some of the other Journey North tele-cornmunications

 

activies.

h) doing some other "creative" experiments and research about Monarchs as I

 

think of them.
