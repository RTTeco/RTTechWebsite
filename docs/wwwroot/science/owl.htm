Title: Owl Pellet Analysis

Purpose: To study the ecosystem within an owl pellet.

Procedure:

1. Place a pellet on a sheet of white paper.

2. Using dissecting needles and forceps, separate the bones of the animals from the fur and/or feathers.

3. Clean the bones of debris and sort them according to type (e.g., skulls, vertebrae, etc.). Clean the skulls as thoroughly as possible since these are the best bones for identifying the prey.

4. Use a key to identify prey mammals.

5. Record the kinds and numbers of prey you find in your pellet on your data sheet (attached) and on a chart on the chalkboard. After the class record is complete, copy the class data onto your record sheet.

6. Answer the questions on the data sheet about the ecology of owls.

Part I : Answers to the Lab Questions

1. If a barn owl produces one pellet each day, the owl would have to eat an estimated 730 animals in one year.

2. Using owl pellets as information about the abundance of all the small animal populations in the owls' habitat would be very inaccurate. By just using the owl pellet for information you do not see the total population of all the animals, but just the small group of animals that that certain owl ate.

3. There are nine species of owls that live in North Dakota. They breed sporadically in North Dakota. They just have one mate for life. They live in trees, barns, caves, belfries, abandoned mine, and bird boxes.

Part II : Answers to the Encarta Questions

1. The sclerotic ring is a capsule of bone that the eves are encases in. This allows very little eye movement, so they must turn their heads to see to their sides.

2. The solid wastes of the owl are removed in the form of regurgitated pellets. The Indigestible portions of their food, such as bones, hair, and feathers are compressed together to form the pellet.

3. All species belonging to the largest genus of owls look much alike, and are differentiated most strongly by their distinctive voices. This is necessary because they look so much alike.

4. An owl is able to fix on their prey by the sounds that the prey animal makes.

5a. The only owl found in the Americas is the great horned owl.

5b. The smallest owl is the elf owl. The elf owl nests in woodpecker holes, and large cacti.

Part III: Additional research on Raptors

Red-Tailed Hawk

Class: Aves

Order: Falconiformes

Family: Accipitridae

Genus and Species: Butes jamaicensis Nineteen to twenty-five inches in length, large broad wings, and short round tail, are some characteristics of the red-tailed hawk. They have whitish breasts, and have a broad band of streaks across its belly. This bird lives mostly in North America, but some go South for the winter.

Mississippi Kite

Class: Aves

Order: Falconiformes

Family: Accipitridae

Genus and Species: Cletinia Mississippiensis Mississippi kites are commonly observed hovering in one spot, where they get their name from. Pointed wings, wing span of nearly three feet and females are usually larger than males. They eat grasshoppers, cicadas, beetles, crickets, and dragonflies. They usually just sweep the insects right out of the air.

Great Horned Owl

Class: Aves Order: Stringiformes Family: Strigidae Genius and Species: Bubo virginianus Range from 18-25 inches in height wing span of 35-55 inches. They are the heaviest and most powerful American owl. They have bright yellow eyes, and a bold white throat bib. They eat rabbits, rats, and mice. Some prey on opossums, muskrats, woodchucks, squirrels, gophers, and meadow mice.

Bald Eagle

Class: Aves Order: Falconiformes Family: Accipitridae Genus and Species: Halideetus leuccocephalus The entire head, throat, and tail are white. Females are larger that the males. Young bald eagles can be confused with the golden eagle. Their wingspan can be up to six feet with the tips round. They are usually found near water where a large quantity of fish live. Their nests can become quite large weighting up to a single ton as they are refurbished and reused year after year.
