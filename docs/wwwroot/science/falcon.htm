American Peregrine Falcon

Falco peregrinus anatum

    The American Peregrine Falcon is a medium sized raptor.  The adult is slate gray in color and its flanks, tail feathers, and wings are black.  Its underside is white or a reddish buffy color that is spotted and barred with black.  American Peregrine Falcons eat other birds.  They do most of their hunting in the air and are able to swoop at speeds of up to 200 miles per hour.  Falcons are found throughout the United States and Canada.
    The American Peregrine Falcon was placed on the endangered species list on June 2, 1970 as the result of a declining population caused by the use of the pesticide, DDT.  The prey of the falcon was being contaminated with this pesticide.  The American Peregrine Falcon was not initially effected by this, but its eggs had soft shells which allowed them to be broken quite easily, thus leading to the endangerment of this species.
    The outlook for the American Peregrine Falcon is quite good.  It was removed from the endangered species list in 1999, and there are numerous organizations working towards the preservation of this magestic bird.
