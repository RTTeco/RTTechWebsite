Lesson Plan: Star Lab #2

Discovering the Stars and More!!

I. Subject and Grade Level: Astronomy, English, Speech, and Social Studies; Grades K-12.

II. Objectives:

a) Students will be able to identify and locate 3 major constellations; Ursa Major, Ursa Minor, and Orion (if too late in the spring, instead of Orion locate Cassiopeia).

b) The Lakota mythology of the Big and Little Dippers will be explored and related to the Greek mythology of Ursa Major and Minor.

c)Students will be able to identify, locate, and understand the Greek mythology of their Zodiac constellation.

d) By creating their own constellation and writing the mythology for their creation, Students will understand the reasons for the mythology of other civilizations. They will aslo be able to identify the location of their constellation, as well as, explain to their clasmates its mythology.

III. Materials:

a) Internet Sites

b) Paper Star Wheels (if not already made, you will need; Star Wheel Pattern, file folders, glue, and scissors for each student).

c) Star Pattern Handout; "Creating a Constellation"

IV. Preparation:

a) A brief explaination of what a constellation is.

b) An explaination of the need and importance of constellations.

c) Reserving Star Lab and/or a cloudless night sky.

 V.Activities

a) Make a Star Wheel

b) Make a Star Finder Holder

c) Internet assignment:

1. Draw the star patterns for the listed constellations. On your drawing, label the major stars.

2. Write a paragraph explaing the Lakota mythology behind the Big and Little Dippers. Write a second paragraph describing the Greek mythology of Ursa Major and Minor. In a third paragraph compare the two sets of myths.

3. Locate and draw your zodiac constellation. On your drawing, label the major stars. Write a paragraph describing the mythology of your zodiac constellation.

4. Using the internet sites provided, explore other major constellations and read about their mythology.

5. Check out your astronomy skills by taking the provided Astronomy Quiz.....practice, practice, as you will need to be able to identify these constellations while in Star Lab.
