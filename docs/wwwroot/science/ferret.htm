Black Footed Ferret

found in: United States Fish & Wildlife Service

 

 

 


found in: Meet the Black Footed Ferret
   

     Black Footed Ferrets have a black face mask, black legs, and a black tipped tail.  They are native to North America.  Humans poisoned prairie dogs, that is why Black Footed Ferrets are endangered. 
     The steps that are being taken to protect the animals are first they caught 200 ferrets by 1991 for breeding.  The second step is they took 1500 free-ranging breeding adults in 10 or more different poputations, and having 30 breeding adults in each population by the year 2010.
     Black Footed Ferrets are found in Arizona, Colorado, Kansas, Motana, Nebraska, New Mexico, North Dakota, Oklahoma, South  Dakota, Texas, Utah, and Wyoming.
