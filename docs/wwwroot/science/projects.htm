Monarch Migration: This link to Journey North provides much information on a number of online biology projects. The biology classes at Richardton High School made and sent Monarch Butterflies to Mexico in October. They are now tracking the progress of their Monarchs and are eagerly awaiting their arrival back to North Dakota in the spring.

Other Monarch Butterfly Sites for research.

Monarch Watch: The University of Kansas Entomology Department's Monarch Butterfly site which contains information on Monarch life cycle, migration, curricula, projects, vocabulary, and other resources.

Tactics and Vectors: "Tactics and Vectors" was established to encourage, coordinate, help analyze from, and report the results of, field studies on flight tactics and navigation of migrating butterflies in North America. The site includes; the Question of the Moment, how to study migration, and a database on butterfly migration.

Santa Cruz Mining Co. Monarch Library Monarch Site: This library contains many links to Monarch sites as well as a map on migration, and a wonderful story about viewing migrating Monarchs. I have found this library to be helpful in many other projects as well...someone else has already done the internet searching for you.

World Wildlife Federation: WWF fact sheets on Monarch Butterflies.

Tulip Garden Project

Richardton High School's Tulip Garden Project

Life Science teacher Larry Rafferty and his class of seventh graders planted over 50 red Emperor Tulips in October. They will watch their tulip garden for signs of sprouting tulips which will hearld the beginning of spring in North Dakota. They will be reporting their observations to Journey North as the winter continues.

Body Science

Body Science breaks down the parts and reveals some of the mysteries of the machine. The amazing brain in particular is examined, both its evolution and the way it controls our view of the world. Students will meet scientists and athletes trying to improve the
body, as well as scientists who say our entire lives are programmed from birth. And two shows on viruses--one on the science of HIV--explain how our bodies defend themselves from constant attack, and what happens when they fail. The Discovery School site includes activity sheets, a store where you can purchase the videos, web links and more.
