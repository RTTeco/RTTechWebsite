Ambystoma Tigrrnum

The Adult tiger salamander is a thick-bodied animal with yellow spots against a black background.  Once in a while there will be one with spots that are tan or olive green. The spots are never in any certain shape, size, or position.  One may be able to tell its origin by the color and pattern of the background and/or spots.  Tigrinum has a large head and a broad rounded snout.  Their eyes are round.  Their belly is usually yellow or olive with dark pigment.  They have about 12-13 coastal grooves.  Males tend to be proportionally longer, with a more compressed tail and longer staleir hind legs then the females.  During the breeding season the males have a swollen vent area.  The larvae have a yellowish green or olive body with the dark spots  and a strip along each side.  They also ghave a whitish belly.  As they grow, specimens tend to be grayish or greenish, and within a few weeks they start to show yellow or tan spots and gradually merge into the patterns of the adult bodies.

Food Habits

Tiger Salamaders food consists of worms, snails, slugs, and insects in the wild.  In captive they have a diet of smaller salamaders, frogs, newborn mice, and baby snakes.  The larvae begin feeding on small crustaceans and insect larvae.  Once they start growing, they will feast on tadpoles, smaller salamder, and small fish.

Reproduction  

Tiger salamaders migrate to breeding ponds in late winter or early spring after a warm rain thaws the grounds surface.  Males arrive earlier than the females, mainly because they live closer to the ponds during the winter months.  To attract a female, males nudge females with his snout to get her ways from other males.  Once away from the males, the male will walk under the females chin, leading her forward.  The female will then nudge the males tail and vent are.  This stimulates the male to deposit his sperm.  This occurs in the night.

The laying of eggs occurs 24-48 hours  after the insemination occurs.  The female lays eggs and attaches them to twigs, grass stems and leaves that have decayed on the bottom of the ground floor.  Each female produces 100-1000 eggs a season.

Behavior

Adult tiger Salamaders live underground for most of the year.  They dig their own burrows, unlike most other species, who use other burrows dug by other animals.  Their burrows have been found 60 cm below the surface.  This allows them to escape the extreme temperatures on the surface.

Habitat

Adult tiger salamaders are terrestrial animals.   They are found in a number of habitats, like forests, grasslands, and marshy areas.  They need to be around ground that can be burrowed in and ponds for breeding.
