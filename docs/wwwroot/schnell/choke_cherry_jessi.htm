American wild cherry:  a North American wild cherry that produces long clusters of small white flowers, followed by dark red or black bitter fruit. Latin name Prunus virginiana

Leaves

  • Alternate, simple, oblong to nearly oval, finely serrated margin, 2 to 4 inches long, dark green above and paler below, minute glands on petiole.

Bark
  • Smooth, gray brown, conspicuous lenticels that develop into shallow fissures, young stems have shallowly peeling, curling layers.
  • Form: Small, upright tree to 25 feet and 6 inches in diameter. It often forms shrubby thickets.
Branch

  • Branches are slender, light brown to gray. They have a strong unpleasant odor when broken.  The buds are 1/3 inch long covered with brownish scales.
Berries

  • Edible but bitter. Popular with birds.

  • When eaten, the cherry produces a choking sensation and is described to be round, shiny, black to crimson cherries.

  •  15 mm in diameter

  • Mature during the months of Juy to October.

 

 
Flowers

  • White, in a loose terminal raceme (3 to 6 inches long), appearing after leaves from May to June.
USES:  Jelly, Wine, food.

The berries are popular with the bird, but humans do eas the berries.  It is a taste that you either like or you don't.

The jelly is very good.  The kitchen will have a very strong smell during the process of making jelly.

Choke Cherry  Jelly

3 1/2 cups choke cherry juice

7 cups sugar

1 tablespoon butter

2 packages liquid certo

____________________________

To make the juice, cook the choke cherries in water that barely covers the cherries, for about 30 minutes. Strain juice through. Combine juice, sugar and butter and bring to a boil, add Certo, return to a boil, boil 1 minute. Pack in sterile jars and seal.
