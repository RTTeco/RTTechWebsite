Magpie

 

 
   
      Magpies are large black and white birds that inhabit open woodlands, savannas, brush covered country stream side growth. Magpies are residents of Alaska and western Canada, South to east-central California, and east to the great plains.

  *These birds are usually found to be nesting individually, but can sometimes be found in loose colonies.  They are social when they are feeding or during the breading season.

 *The magpie is an omnivorous bird which feeds on insects, snails, slugs, lower rodents, eggs of passerines, pigeons, chicks of poultry or wild birds, and fruit.
   
 *The Females lay eggs between mid-April and mid-May and rear only one brood of 6-8 young during the course of the year.
   
 *This mischievous bird also plunders the nests of several other bird species and can cause damage to crops.  However, this damage is generally unimportant owing to the low density of populations but the numbers are increasing. 
   
 Adult- 41 to 42 cm long 
          48 to 53 cm wingspan
             Feathers black and white, contrasting with a long metallic tail
