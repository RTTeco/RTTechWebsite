Characteristics:

    Length: 4 inches

    Small, active, insect-eating bird

    Thin, pointed bill

    Mostly yellow plumage

    Upperparts greenish-yellow

    Greenish-yellow wings and tail with yellow feather edges

    Plain yellow face with yellow eye ring surrounding dark eye

    Yellowish legs

Cool fact: Yellow Warblers have an extraordinarily broad distribution for a warbler species and show great geographical variation. More than 40 recognized subspecies form three general groups that range from the northern limits of shrubby habitat in Canada to northern South America.

    Yellow Warblers are fairly large warblers, with relatively short tails. Upper parts are greenish yellow and under parts are yellow. Males have chestnut streaks on the upper breast and flanks. Females are more dull yellow with greenish crowns, and they exhibit faint breast streaking, if any. The yellow tail spots, visible on the underside, are distinctive among warblers (female and immature American Redstarts have yellow patches at the base of the tail). The head and face are unmarked in northern Yellow Warblers, with just a hint of chestnut wash in some males, making the dark eye stand out. Little seasonal change in plumage occurs.
