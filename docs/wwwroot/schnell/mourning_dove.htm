Habitat & Migratory Habits

 Mourning doves are the most abundant game bird in the Southeast.  They are primarily farm game birds that thrive where grain crops are grown.  Because the dove is a migratory species, local habitat conditions generally do not limit the population, nor will manipulation of the environment increase populations.  They usually nest in trees along the edges of fields or pastures.

Food

A dove's diet consists almost entirely of seeds from cultivated fields and weeds along fence rows. 

Key Foods

Corn, Rye, ProsoMillet, Sudan Grass, Croton, Crab Grass, Sedges, Wild Peas, Evening Browntop, Primrose Millet, Wheat, Pine Seed, Sunflower, Crowfoot, and Poke Weed.

Life Span

Doves reproduce fast, but have a short life span.  They usually live for less than a year.

Breeding

Males and Females are believed to be mates for life, and they reproduce at high rates.

Fun Facts

The mourning dove is named for its soft, low-pitched call.

Water is a main requirement for doves.

Length:  10.5 inches

Sexes are similar
