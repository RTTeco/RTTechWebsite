The Virginia, or white-tailed, deer, Odocoileus virginianus, is found throughout North and Central America, where it winters in conifer swamps and summers at the outer edges of forests, and as far south as Bolivia, where it divides its time between river valleys and higher ground. It has also been introduced in New Zealand and parts of Scandinavia. White-tailed deer have a reddish summer coat that fades to gray-brown in the colder months. The white patches on its underside give the animal its name.
