BLUE JAY

"Blue Jay" is the common name for a familiar bird of central  and eastern North America, allied to the crow, the raven, and the magpie, belonging to the family Corvidae.  Blue Jays are almost a foot long and are handsome and conspicious. 

Their upper parts, including the crest, are grayish violet blue.  The wings and tail are are bright blue with black and white markings.  They feed chiefly on large insects, seeds, and nuts.  They also eat eggs and nestlings.  When the female Blue Jay is incubating, she is fed by the males.
