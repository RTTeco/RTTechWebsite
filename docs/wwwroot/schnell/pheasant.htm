Ring-necked Pheasant

The ring-necked pheasant has a long and pointed tail.  Male pheasants have a red eye patch, a green head, and a white neck ring.  The body of the pheasant is a soft brown color.  The voice of a ring-necked pheasant is a loud crowing sound.  Their habitat is found in farmlands, pastures, and grassy woodland edges.  Adults eat berries, seeds, buds, and leaves, while the chicks eat mainly insects.
