Mink

  \\\\
Minks are semi-aquatic meat eaters. Mainly nocturnal, it feeds on fish, frogs, and shellfish in the water, and on land they feed on birds, and small mammals.

They have a slender body covered by a soft, thick coat of fur, that is valuable to the fur industry.

They live in burrows 3 m long in the river bank.

During the mating season, they will live in sheltered burrows.  2-10 young are born in each litter.  They may live up to 10 years.

Classification

  • Kingdom: Animalia      

  • Phylum: Chordata        

  • Class: Mammalia         

  • Order: Carnivora         

  • Family: Mustelidae      

  • Subfamily: Mustelinae

  • Genus Mustela             

  • Species: Mustela vison
