American Mink

By Chad Hueske

    This aquatic member of the weasel family is found throughout most of northern North America and the most commercially valuable fur-bearer. Large males may reach a total length of 60 cm. As adults they are bold, ferocious, and virtually untamable, but if they are captured as kittens they are playful and can become attached to the person who cares for them. 
     The mink is a skillful hunter and preys on a wide variety of game including muskrats, meadow voles, and cottontails, as well as fish, crayfish, and frogs. Its own principal enemies are the great horned owl, bobcats, wolves, and coyotes. 
     A solitary, mainly nocturnal animal, the mink is active throughout the year.
