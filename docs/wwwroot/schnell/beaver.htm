(Castor Canadensis)

Description:

The beaver is the largest rodent in North America.  They are non migratory and live by water year round.  Beavers are relatively short and squat, with a large rump.  Each foot has five toes, with the hind feet being webbed to help the beaver groom.  Beavers have small, short ears and have been known to stand using their hind feet only.  Typically the tail is used to help balance the beaver while they perform this maneuver.

Habitat:

Beavers are almost entirely aquatic.  While beavers are most home in water, they are quite capable of traveling on land, and do so regularly to reach nearby trees.  Beavers are slow and awkward on land though, and are not able to move as gracefully.  A single pond will consist of a family group living in burrows in stream banks or in lodges of mud, stones, and tree branches.  These lodges are built by the beavers themselves.

Food:

Despite the large front teeth of the beaver, these animals are herbivores.  They eat plants only.  Most commonly they eat the bark of certain hardwoods such as poplar, aspen, birch, cherry, willow, maple, and alder.  Aspen and pond weeds are their favorite natural foods.  Among domesticated beavers, or beavers that have been raised by humans, they have been known to eat a number of fruits such as apples.

Reproduction:

Beavers are monogamous but if one mate dies, the other will "remarry".  Family groups consist of two adults, several two year olds, and the young of the current year.  They mate first at about three years old.  Gestation is 128 days.  Litters of 2 to 6 are born in April and May.  Kits can swim when they are a few hours old, and are weaned at one month.  The mother carries the kits in her mouth supported on her front legs while walking upright on her hind legs and tail.  Young leave or are forced out of the colony in two years.  Large lodges may have several family songs.  Family life is cooperative.

Fun Facts:

-Beavers can cut down trees with up to 30 inch diameters.

-In 1800, the beaver was extinct east of the Mississippi.  In some places the beaver has re-established itself to nuisance proportions.

-They can cut trees down faster than some grown men.  (8" diameter in 5 minutes)

-Beavers can hold their breath for 20 minutes.
