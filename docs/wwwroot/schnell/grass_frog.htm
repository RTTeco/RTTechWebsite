The Grass Frog

(Rana temporaria)

Amphibian typical of high mountain areas, living above 600 m and up to 2,600 m in altitude, at the upper limit of the subalpine forests, in alpine meadows and marshes.

It can be recognised by the reddish, or often black, colouring of its dorsal parts, contrasting highly with its very pale underside. Behind the eye, above the ear, is a dark, highly visible, triangular patch. Although this frog is not very large (up to 10 cm), it looks very strong and muscular.

 

It is relatively abundant in el Ripollès and la Cerdanya in places where there is slow-moving water such as in marshes and at the edges of lakes and streams. It is also found in localized spots in the regions of la Garrotxa and l'Alt Empordà. A small remnant group still exists in the area of Montseny, which is of great biogeographical value.

Mating:
Often already early March.
Common frogs spawn explosively: within 2-3 days many animals spawn, often hundreds of them together, during constant, soft growling of the males. The egg clumps usually float at the water surface.
This frog is the most common one we have.
Except for the breeding season Common frogs have a hidden life on land.
