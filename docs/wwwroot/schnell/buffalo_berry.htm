Buffalo Berry

This plant can be considered either a large shrub or a small tree.  It can tolerate poor sites and is extremely hardy.  The limbs of the shrub are thorny, and it produces a scarlet fruit.  These berries that are created can be used in jellies or jams and can be dried or eaten raw.  They seem to have a tart taste, but after a frost, they tend to sweeten a bit.
   

The plant is dioecious, which means a single plant will bear only male or female flowers.  Male plants have dense clusters of rounded flower buds, and the female plants have fewer buds that are smaller, flatter, and more slender.  The male plants are needed for pollination, and the females are the only ones to produce the fruit.  It is said that for each four to five female plants, one male plant is needed.
   

 Buffalo berries are mainly used for jelly and sauces.  They can be eaten right off the bush, but they do have a very tart taste. 

 Here's a recipe for buffalo berry spice sauce:

4 cups buffalo berries
Grated rind of one orange
2 cups water
2 cups sugar
1/4 tsp. ground cinnamon
Pinch of ground cloves

Combine orange rind, water, and sugar in a saucepan.  Cook over moderate heat for 10 minutes, then add berries.  Cook till berries pop.  Add cinnamon and cloves and cook for 5 minutes while stirring frequently.  Spoon mixture into a bowl and place into refrigerator to chill.  Serve chilled.  Makes a red colored, spicy sauce best served with meat.
