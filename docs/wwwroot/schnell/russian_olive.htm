Russian Olive

 A member of the Oleaster family, the Russian olive is a shrub or a small tree that have elliptic leaves.  Its branches are thorny, and its fruit is yellow, dry, and mealy.  Russian olive may be found in dry to moist soils but does well in sandy flood plains.  They can also be found along streams, fields, and open areas.

Russian olive can outcompete native vegetation.  It's capable of fixing nitrogen in its roots; therefore, it can grow on bare, mineral substrates and dominate riparian vegetation.  The Russian-olive provides a plentiful source of edible fruits for birds.  These are a small cherry-like drupe that is eaten by many species of birds.
 

 The Russian-olive was first cultivated in Germany in 1736 and was introduced to the US. in the late 1800s.  It was planted as an ornamental and subsequently escaped into the wild.  
 

It reproduces primarily by seed, and some vegetation propagation also occurs.
