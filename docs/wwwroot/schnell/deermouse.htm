Deer Mouse
 

 
   
 *The Deer mouse lives in North America and is distributed from the northern tree line in Alaska and Canada southward to central Mexico. 
   
 *Peromyscus maniculatus, as it is also called, has a small body no longer than that of a house mouse.  The tail length, however, is variable in different populations and ranges from 45 mm to 105 mm.  The woodland forms are generally larger than that of the prairie.  The hind limbs are longer than the forelimbs.
   
 *The Deer mouse is omnivorous which eats a wide variety of plant and animal matter depending on what is available, including insects and other invertebrates, seeds, fruits, flowers, nuts, and other plant products. 
   
 *It is primarily a nocturnal species and night hunting predators, such as snakes and owls, are its biggest threat.  Their activity centers around a nest and food cache.
   
 *The Deer mouse is an abundant species, often among the most abundant mouse species of certain areas.  Densities can reach 11 mice per acre.  The quantity and quality of foods, availability of water, number and distribution of nest sites, architecture of living and dead vegetation are some of the ecological factors proposed to effect the density of  the deer mice.
