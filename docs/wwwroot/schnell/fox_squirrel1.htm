Fox Squirrel

Isn't it cute?
...Fox squirrels are  found in the eastern and central parts of the United States, northern Mexico, and southern Canada.  They have been introduced to western North America. 
FOod:  Moths, beetles, bird eggs, dead fish, acorn, hickory, walnut, mulberry, hawthorn seeds.

Food is scarce during the winter, so they cache seeds in a scattered fashion.  Nuts are opened by a levering technique of the lowering incisors.
Reproduction: Squirrels can reproduce anytime during the year.  Intercourse lasts less than 30 seconds.  Pregnancy lasts 44 - 45 days.  Average size of a litter is 2-3, but can range up to 7.  Young are born naked, weighing 13-18 gm, eyes open at 5 weeks, and are weaned at 8.  Sexual maturity is reached at 8 months for females, and 10-11 months for males.  Females can produce 2 litter a year, but 1 is normal.
Habitat: Fox squirrels are found in deciduos and mixed forests.

They are great for Humans

Squirrels are hunted as a food source  and their fur.  They are important, because they spread seeds and bury nuts for trees.

They are bad for Humans

Squirrels are a nuisance to people who own bird feeder.  They raid the feeders and gardens.  They use electrical lines for traveling and cause power outages.
