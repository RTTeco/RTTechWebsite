Habitat & Environment

   The skunk prefers open areas of mixed forests and grasslands and has very little fear of humans, so it has benefited from the new habitats created by the opening of the forests that accompanied settlement and agriculture.

   They generally live in the abandoned dens of woodchucks, foxes, or other mammals their size or larger and only occasionally excavate their own dens.  Rock piles, stumps, or refuse heaps can also be used.

Food

THey eat insects, mice, shrews, ground squirrels, young rabbits, birds' eggs, and various plants.  THey eat mainly insects in the summer.  THey are especially fond of grasshoppers, crickets, and insect larvae such as white grubs, army worms, and cutworms.
