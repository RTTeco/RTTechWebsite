THE FIREMOUSE TWO

Objective: To learn the difference between potential and kinetic energy. Work independently or cooperatively. Work with only assigned materials. Work within a given limit of time. To learn to use different forces or prime movers. To learn how energy transfers work. To learn to brainstorm and develop problem solving skills. Make decisions using trial and error.

Problem: Given the approved materials, independently extinguish a standard birthday candle, pop a air filled water balloon, and raise a flag beginning with the potential and kinetic energy of a mousetrap. 

Limits: One day of planning (homework).
Work in groups of no more than two.
Each group will have an allotted time limit to set up.
Each group will have as many attempts as time allows or candle allows.
The candle must remain standing after it is extinguished.
 The flag must be raised off the base and remain raised.
Any one item within the Firemouse Two cannot have a value of over $2.00.
The contents of the firemouse or material used must be safe (Instructors discretion).
The Firemouse must be mobile.

Given: One base plate approximately 20.32cm x 111.76cm
One standard mousetrap
One birthday candle
One flag on a staff
One water balloon
The set up: Mousetrap and candle are placed on base plate.
A hole may be drilled to fit the candle.

The candle will be lit and allowed to burn for 15 seconds before the firemouse is activated.

Evaluation 1: The operator gets one chance to put out the candle, raise the flag, and pop the balloon for a total of 25 points.
                    Each additional attempt the operator will be penalized three points
