Design Department

Introduction

The Design Department is responsible for developing a project to manufacture. The members of this department should review the Criteria for Project Selection. The members should then choose a number of potential manufacturing ideas. The design department will work in conjunction with all other departments. Other duties of this department include:

·Thumbnail sketches
·A drawing of each jig and fixture
·Drawing of the finished product
·A drawing of the assembled package

 Criteria for Project Selection

1.                  The project should be a product that is or could be produced commercially.

2.                  The product should be a product that interests the manufacturing team.

3.                  The product should be an item that is adaptable to mock industrial operations in the school lab.

4.                  The product should involve the desired mechanical applications.

5.                  The product should possess room for development and improvement.

6.                  The product should have commercial value and sell on its own merit.

7.                  The required operations in the product development must be within the capabilities of the team involved.

8.                  The standard stock or standard part materials must be readily available.

9.                  The product should lend itself to a breakdown of twelve to eighteen steps (a little less for smaller team sizes). A product that is too simple with only one or two setups or workstations does not give an adequate representation of industrial production.

10.              The product should give the team members opportunity to work with a variety of material.

11.              The run should be long enough that the team can develop some proficiency at his/her job.

12.              The cost per item should be small.

13.              The product must be functional.

Develop several thumbnail sketches of your ideas during brainstorming sessions.

·A working drawing
·A pictorial drawing
·A assembled drawing
·A exploded view drawing
·Any enlarged detail drawings required

Fabrication Department

Introduction

The Fabrication Department in conjunction with Production Department is responsible for departmental safety and the designing all jigs and fixtures.

·Design and implement a safety program
·Design jigs and fixtures
· Measure materials, calculate numbers for production
·Select best materials
·Materials list
·Develop the proper route sheets
·Develop a flow chart using the laboratory floor plan
·Develop the scheduling needed for inventory control

Task One

Design and Implement a Safety Program

Have all representatives of the organization work with a representative from the Production Department, to design and develop a safety program for each area of Fabrication and Production departments.

Develop safety posters for each area of fabrication and production. Analyze the safety aspect of each area of production and fabrication. Obtain all materials (paper, poster board, markers, glue, etc.) needed for this assignment.

On an 8.5x11 inch sheet of paper, list safety rules, and design a safety poster that will. Graphically communicate one or more of your items. The poster must include at least one graphic that demonstrates a safety concern. The graphic may be clipart, digital picture, or an original art. Make a sketch to serve as your draft copy and submit to the instructor for approval.

When approved by the instructor, make the real poster on the material provided.

Post all safety posters in their working area. Although it is possible to create a computer generated layout, the computers needed for this may be already in use. Therefore, this activity is intended to be a hand drawn poster, colored with markers or pencils.

(Optional) Produce a video dealing with a safety issue within your organization

Task Two

Design jigs and fixtures

Working with a representative from the Production department, you will design all necessary jigs and fixtures for the fabrication of the final product.

With the instructor design any jig and fixtures to hold the material. Each piece of the product will need to be analyzed. Look at the part and decide what kind of jig or fixture will be needed to make this particular piece. Ask the representative from the production department if the part can be made on their machine.

Make this jig/fixture and test it.

(Ask for instructor assistance in construction. of jigs and fixtures)

Make adjustments to the jig and fixture as needed.

Task Three

Measure Materials, Calculate Numbers for Production

Measure and layout material to make sure you have enough stock for the project.

Collect construction materials and the rough material component list from instructor. Produce a design sketch and give it to the Design Department.

Using measurement instruments, determine the amount of the materials. Needed to complete individual parts from the product design sketch. Keep your records in your daily log or student note book.

Calculate the number of products to be completed. Count all students in the classroom, Administrators, Supervisors, Superintendent, School Board members and others as directed by the instructor to arrive at a total, Record this number.

Determine the amount of material needed to make each product and multiply by the quantity needed. Add for any extras if desired. Record the total amount of each material needed.

Layout the size and shape of the product on the materials.

Task Four

Select Best Materials

Select best part of the material to eliminate any imperfections before cutting into correct sizes.

Review the list of defects of material. (Ask instructor for posted defects)

Identify any defects on your chosen materials.

Adjust the layout of measurements from the above task to eliminate defects in the final product.

Task Five

Materials List

Revise the materials list and check to make sure that enough materials will be available. .

Check drawings of product plan to ensure correct amount of material.

Make a list of materials (revised) of which you are short to complete all of the products. Inform the instructor of your fist.

Task Six

Develop the proper route sheets. There will be a separate page for each part manufactured. There will be a page for each subassembly. Use the route sheets furnished by your instructor.

Task Seven

Develop a flow chart using the laboratory floor plan. Show the pathways of manufacturing process with the floor plan of the laboratory you are using.

Task Eight

Develop the scheduling needed for inventory control. Develop a spreadsheet to keep the manufacturing process going. Tag components in storage. Keep track of production to avoid overruns.

·Separating
·Forming

·Assembling

Task One

Process Materials

Process materials to the correct size. Instructor should be present if power tools. Need to be used. .

With the instructor present, review the process of operating machines to safely (review safety posters). Review cleanup procedures with instructor.

Only with the instructor present, operate machinery, to convert raw material- into product parts. Keep track of the number of parts you make. Make sure work area is cleaned-up everyday. Allow sufficient time for straightening up.

Task Two

Assemble Product

Assemble product and complete all finishing work.

Separate parts of the product into different sections.

Assemble parts according to the sketch.

Check with instructor for special cleanup and storage requirements.

Complete the product, apply any finish required.

Quality checks each product.

Send the final product to the Packaging Department.

Task Three (optional)

Develop a video on how this product was made

Packaging Department

Introduction

The Packaging Department is responsible for designing the package that will contain the product and all materials that will be packaged with the product. This will be done in the following steps:

·.Design the package
·Develop a prototype of the package for final review
·Develop a working sketch to submit to the Design Department for final drawings.
·Develop the exterior design- (decals and stickers) of the package and work in conjunction with the Marketing Department to get-these items produced.
·Develop a materials list to submit to the Design Department.
·Develop a production plan for the assembly of the package.

Assemble the package

·Package the product with all forms and instructions included.

Task One

Design the Package

With the group leader in charge and using the prototype, go through the problem solving process to design a package for our product. The package should meet the following criteria:

·The package should be primarily a folded package. We want to keep gluing to a minimum. Look for alternate packaging like recycling small boxes
·You must use the materials provided. By your instructor or go through him to add anything that is not already available.
·Be sure that the package is large enough to hold the product and any materials that we may add to be packaged with it.
·It should not be too large, because we want our product to be stable in the package so that it is not damaged in shipping. We also want to be as efficient as possible with our materials.
·The final design should be submitted to your instructor for final approval in sketch form.

Task Two

Develop a Prototype

After the package has been selected, a full sized layout must be developed and folded into the final package.        .

Obtain the package material from you instructor

Using drafting tools supplied by your instructor layout the package. Be sure that all of your lines are straight and at proper angles. Dont forget to put glue tabs where necessary. (It may even be possible to make a template for package layout.)

Fold your package to make sure that it works perfectly.

Submit your prototype to your instructor for final approval.

Task Three

Develop a Working Sketch

Take the approved prototype of your package that you made. Unfold it so that you can lay it flat on.-the table.

Using a ruler measure the exact dimensions and write them on the prototype.

Create a working sketch complete with all dimensions.

Submit your working sketch to the Design Department.

Task Four

Develop the Exterior Design of the Package

Now your group must design the exterior stickers and decals that will go on the outside of the package. The exterior of the package should contain the following items.

·The Company Name, Address, Phone Number, Web Address, etc.
·The Company Logo (possible company picture)
·A graphic of the product
·Text and other graphics that describe the product
·Bar Code
·Recycle symbol
·Shipping Weight
·Made with pride in USA
·The name of your product in an alternate language would also boost your customer availability.

With the group leader in charge, go through the problem solving process to decide on how the exterior of the package should look. Remember that in many cases the package sell the product.

When your group is finished the group leader will meet with the Marketing Department and make sure that they are capable of producing your design.

Final design must be submitted to the instructor for final approval before the Marketing Department to begin to produce the necessary items.

Task Five

Develop a Bill of Materials

A Bill of Materials is an organized list of everything that you will need to complete a given project. We are going to compile and produce a Bill of Materials for our package.

List all of the materials that will be needed to complete the package including all tools. This list is to include all things that you are going to use.

Break the list into the following areas:

· Materials needed for the package itself (including sizes).
·Materials needed for the exterior of the package (including sizes)
·Any tools, paint, glue, etc. needed to assemble the package.
·Group leader should consult with the instructor to create an acceptable format for the Bill of Materials.

Produce the final Bill of Materials. Submit it to the instructor for final approval. Then submit the approved product to the Design Department.

Task Six

Develop a Production Plan

Now we must develop a plan that will include everybody that will allow us to assemble our package in an organized and efficient manner.

With the group leader in charge, list all of the necessary steps to assemble the package.

Get all of the steps in sequence. What has to come first, second, etc?

Assign group members to specific jobs. Review the process and eliminate all bugs.

Task Seven

Assemble the Packages

Put the production plan into action. Anticipate a few unexpected production problems at first. Dont panic. This is normal.

Begin Assembly

Deal with any unexpected problems.

Be sure that the group stays on task. Exercise quality control at all stages.

Assemble the required number of packages.

Task Eight

Pack the Finished Products for Shipping

Now we will package the product for final shipping.

Obtain finished products from the Fabrication Department.

Obtain any forms, flyers, evaluations, etc. from the Marketing Department.

Pack all materials for shipping.

Dissolution Essay

Manufacturing Technology

This narrative will be your closing and your groups process of dissolution of your organization. Each person in your group will submit all information even if it is duplicated.

Included in your narrative you should write about the manufacturing experience you had. Write about what you did and how you benefited the organization. Write about not only the successes of the organization but also the problems that were encountered (including items like tooling up, jigs and fixtures, tolerances).  What changes you would do if you would set up a similar organization. List the success of the organization, or the short falls. Include the amount invested and the amount of profit. Did you have any doubts that this might not be as successful as it was? Was it risky? Write about a few new concepts you learned through this team effort and about manufacturing so far. Write about how well you worked with your co-workers. How well did they work with you? Who was your supervisor? Who did you supervise? If you were to go work in the manufacturing industry, which practice would you like to pursue (management, personnel, or production). Why?

If you had an opportunity to use any high technology tool, process or processes in the making of the product you made, what would it be?

How would it be implemented?

If you were part of another organization, how did this one compare to the previous one.

Your essay will be neatly typed or word processed, There is no maximum or minimum for length but in must be complete.
