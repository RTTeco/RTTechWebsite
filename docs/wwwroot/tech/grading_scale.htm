What is a Grade
A grade is an inadequate report of an inaccurate judgment by a biased and variable judge of the extent to which a student has obtained in undefined level of mastery of an unknown proportion of an indefinite amount of material.
(attributed to Dr. Robert Hanson, author of the very popular high school textbook, century 21 bookkeeping)

A

Excellent achievement. Student performance demonstrates thorough knowledge of the course materials and exceeds
course expectations by completing all requirements in a superior manner

B

Very good work. Student performance demonstrates above-average comprehension of the course materials and exceeds course expectations on all tasks as defined in the course syllabus.

C

Student performance meets designated course expectations and demonstrates understanding of the course materials at an acceptable level.

D

Marginal work. Student performance demonstrates incomplete understanding of course materials.

D-

Unsatisfactory work. Student performance demonstrates incomplete and inadequate understanding of course materials.

F

Unacceptable work. Student has inadequate understanding of course materials. Reasons may include  incomplete assignments, assignments not handed in, student not attending class.

The grading scale used by Mr. Rohwedder in all of the Technology Education classes follow the recommended grading scale used by Power School.

The categories for the Technology Classes and 7th grade Geography are as follows:

Classroom observance - 10%

Punctuality - 10%

Time Management, Work Ethic and Conduct - 15%

Assignments}
Module Rotations}  - 40%
Homework}

Tests - 25%

Assignments and test grades are weighted more as the year progresses. This will allow for a student
that begins the class or struggles in the beginning to be successful at the end of the class.

Unfortunately the reverse can happen if a student falls behind or gets negligent at the end of class.

Semester grades are calculated by averaging the previous two quarters.

Late assignments will be accepted. A reduction of points will be subtracted from the total for each day the assignment is late.
