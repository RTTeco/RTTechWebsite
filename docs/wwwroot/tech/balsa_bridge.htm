Objective: To develop planning skills relative to the TSA standards.
                  To Demonstrate problem solving skills.
                  Use technology to solve problems.
                  Use research and experimentation procedures to develop new processes and procedures.
                  Describe industrial impacts on our environment.
                  Use applied research to investigate forces such as tension, twist, and compression.
                  To learn the difference between materials and their properties.
                  To learn to use one type of bonding agent efficiently.
                  Work independently or cooperatively.
                  Work with only assigned materials.
                  Work within a given limit of time.

Problem:    To design and build the lightest bridge capable of supporting a given load over a span of 325 mm (12 5/8 inches). Using a given amount of material (21 feet of 1/8" square balsa wood).

Limits:          One day or less of planning (homework).
    Bridge to be completed in 5 class periods.
    No use of power tools.
    Use only the materials given.
    You will not receive any more.
    You will not be allowed to start over.
    Bridge must be free standing.
    Road bed must not be more that 30 mm above the test device.
    Bridge must at least hold the minimum of the testing hook (120 grams 4.45 oz)
    Bridge not be taped to the test device.
    Bridge must stand for 10 seconds during the testing time period only.
    Bridge construction and testing will take one period of class time.
    Work in groups of no more than two.
    No hot or cool melt glue.

Given:            7 36 inch 1/8" x 1/8" balsa square stock (21 feet).
     White glue.
     Access to scissor, xacto knife.

Variables:      Bridge must be minimum of span of 13.5 inches (340 mm).
     Bridge width of 3 inches (76 mm).

Subjective judgment
The instructor may award 1-10 points for creative and engineering solutions. This score will be added to the actual performance of the bridge to determine the grade. In case of a tie, the bridge that is the lightest will be declared the winner.
