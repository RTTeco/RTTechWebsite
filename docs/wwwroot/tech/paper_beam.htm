Context:    Beam are used to form the skeletons for many types of structures (bridges, skyscaperers, towers, etc). The size and weight of these beams is not nearly as important as their load carring capacities. With proper design, even light weight materials may be sometimes be fabricated to produce beams with considerable strength.

Objective: To learn the difference between materials and their properties.
                   To learn to use one type of bonding agent efficiently.
                   Work independently or cooperatively.
                   Work with only assigned materials.
                   Work within a given limit of time.

Problem:   Given one sheet of standard paper (8.5 x11 inches) and white glue, construct a Beam that will support the most amount of weight. The beam will span a distance of 8 inches.

Limits:          One day or less of planning (homework).
    No use of power tools.
    Use only the materials given.
    You will not receive any more.
    You will not be allowed to start over.
    Beam must be free standing.
    Beam not be taped to the test equipment.
    Beam must support the weight for 15 seconds during the testing time period only.
    When the Beam fails the last weight that is applied is not counted in the total weight.
    Beam construction and testing will take one period or less to of class time.
    Work in groups of no more than two.

Given:             One sheet of standard paper 8.5 x 11 inches
       Access to scissor, xacto knife, and white glue.

Evaluation:    A - 20 points -The Beam that supported the most weight.
                       B -18 points -The Beam that supported 75% of the  Beam that supported the most weight.
                       C -17 points -The Beam that supported 50% of the  Beam that supported the most weight.
                       C -16 points -The Beam that supported 25% of the  Beam that supported the most weight.
                       D -15 points -The Beam that supported 10% of the  Beam that supported the most weight.
                       F - 11 points - Tried but no Beam submitted.
                       F - 0 points - No Beam submitted.
