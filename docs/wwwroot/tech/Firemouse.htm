THE FIREMOUSE

Objective: To learn the difference between potential and kinetic energy. Work independently or cooperatively. Work with only assigned materials. Work within a given limit of time. To learn to use different forces or prime movers. To learn how energy transfers work. To learn to brainstorm and develop problem solving skills. Make decisions using trial and error.

Problem:   Given the approved materials, extinguish a standard birthday candle with the potential and kinetic energy of a mousetrap placed on a given support one meter apart.

Limits:            One day of planning (homework).
      Work in groups of no more than two.
      Each group will have an allotted time limit to set up (no more than a class period).
      Each group will have up to four attempts as time allows or candle allows.
      The candle must remain standing after it is extinguished.
      Group will be limited to the material given only, no substitutes.
      The operation of the Firemouse must work independently on its own once the mouse trap is activated.

The candle will be lit and allowed to burn for 15 seconds before the firemouse is activated.

Given:             One base plate approximately 20.32cm x 111.76cm
      One standard mousetrap
      One birthday candle
      One meter of string
      White glue
      One large soda straw
      One small soda straw
       Three 3” x 5” index cards
       2 sheets of standard paper (8.5” x 11”)
       Ten centimeters of tape, (masking or clear)
       Hot glue only to secure mousetrap to base plate

The set up:        Mousetrap and candle are placed one meter apart on base plate. 
        A hole may be drilled to fit the candle.
        Mousetrap can be place in either direction toward the candle.

Evaluation grades will be determined by the following. In three categories.

Evaluation 1: The operator gets one chance to put out the flame without penalty. Then each attempt is penalized, minus three points. If the candle flame is put out on the first try, 25 points are awarded.
