The Mamba is one of the tallest, longest, fastest roller coasters in the world. It is a steel track roller coaster that was opened in 1998. It features a 580 degree rotation at speeds of 60 mph. There are five "camelback" bumps including an unusual "double-up" bump. The Mamba is ranked number 18 in the world among steel coasters. The Mamba is located at the Worlds of Fun amusement park in Kansas City, Missouri. The designer of this thrill ride was D.H. Morgan Manufacturing Inc. from La Selva Beach, California.

Height of first drop: 205 feet

Height of second hill: 184 feet

Speed: 75+ mph

Length of Track: 5,600 feet

Ride duration: approximately 3 minutes

Capacity: 1,700 riders per hour

Maximum G-force: 3.5

Cost: $10 million

 

 

 

 

 

Worlds of Fun is an amusement park located in Kansas City, Missouri.  They have a number of rides including two steel roller coasters, the Mamba and the Orient Express, a wooden coaster, the Timber Wolf, along with a large number of other thrill rides.  Worlds of Fun opening 30 years ago.  It costs $35 for one day of unlimited riding.
