Characteristics

    Bees eat nectar from flowers.  Bees are dependent on pollen as a protein source and on flower nectar or oils as an energy source.   They have biting chewing mouth parts and two pairs of wings.  Bees have feather like hairs that help in collecting pollen.  Weather conditions affect both flowering plants and bees.  Early spring weather is good for flowering plants but is bad for bees, while the dry weather is what the bees prefer.  Bees life span is approximately a few weeks.  Male bees use the plumes on their middle legs to cover the eyes of the females during mating, so female bees won't get spooked.  There are about 20,000 species of bees worldwide.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do bug zappers really work?     Bug zappers have been claimed to provide relief from mosquitoes and other pests.  A few studies have shown that bug zappers are very poor at killing mosquito females.  A study indicated that bug zappers were worst than worthless because of the large number of harmless and beneficial insects that they kill. What to do when beetles are in your flour?     Red flour beetle originates from infested grain, flour, cake mix, crackers, dry pet food, and so forth.  To control any stored food pest requires locating and eliminating the infested items.  All food s that could be potentially infested should be checked.  You should empty and clean cupboards or shelves.  Empty the vacuum cleaner after you use it.  As a precaution you should store susceptible foods in glass, metal, or heavy plastic containers that are sealable.   Insect Recipes  

    Banana Worm Bread

Ingredients:                                            

1/2 cup shortening
3/4 cup sugar
2 bananas, mashed
2 cups flour
1 teaspoon soda
1 teaspoon salt
1/2 cup chopped nuts
2 eggs
1/4 cup dry-roasted army worms

Directions:                                        Mix all ingredients together.  Bake in grease loaf pan at 350 degrees for about 1 hour.                      Iowa State

Chocolate Chirpy Chip Cookies

Ingredients:

2 1/4 cup flour
1 tsp. baking soda
1 tsp. salt
1 cup butter, softened
3/4 cup sugar
3/4 cup brown sugar
1 tsp. vanilla
2 eggs
1 12-ounce chocolate chips
1 cup chopped nuts
1/2 cup dry-roasted crickets

Directions:                                                                                                      Preheat oven to 375.  In a small bowl, combing flour, baking soda, and salt; set aside.  In a large bowl, combine butter, sugar, brown sugar, and vanilla; beat until creamy.  Beat in eggs.  Add flour and insects.  Mix well.  Stir in chocolate chips.  Drop by rounded measuring teaspoonfuls onto ungreased cookie sheet.  Bake for 8-10 minutes. Iowa State

Poke- A -Dot Rambo

    Poke-a-Dot Rambo, Orbis Miles Militis, has two pairs of wings.  Its large wings enable it to fly thousands of feet above the ground.  It can also swim.  Its six legs allow it to swim through the tropical rivers.  Poke-a-Dot Rambo is brightly colored to scare away its predators.  It has chewing mouth parts, and it pollinates tropical flowers.  

    Poke-a- Dot Rambo is classified in the order hymenoptera.  It lives in tropical areas.  It lives in tropical areas because it must keep moist to breathe.    It undergoes complete metamorphosis.  It eats the nectar of the tropical flowers.  Snakes and birds are its main predators.  It scares away its predators by its brightly colored poke a dots.  To defend itself, it has a golden stinger.
