  Coleoptera 

          Coleoptera is the largest order in the class Insecta.  The most known insects in this order are beetles.  As adults, most beetles have a hard, dense exoskeleton that covers and protects most of their body surface.  The front wings, known as elytra, are just as hard as the rest of the exoskeleton.  They fold down over the abdomen and serve as protective covers for the large hind wings.  Both larvae and adults have strong mandible mouthparts.  Many of the species of beetles are herbivores.  Some species live on fungi, others burrow into plant tissues, and others excavate tunnels in wood or under bark.

Interimo Volucris (Killer Beetle)

The Interimo Volucris, better known as the Killer Beetle, is a very dangerous bug.  The sharp antenna like structures inject a poison into anything it stabs into.  The Killer Beetle has heat censored eyespots so it can sort of see where it is going.  The beetle has a hard outer body covering used mostly for protection.  They have three pairs of walking legs.  Each leg has hundreds of little hair like structures that help the insect move fast. 

    The Killer Beetle is in the order coleoptera.  It's habitat consists of tropical areas.  The sharp antenna like structures are adapted to the environment because their are many predators in the area that it lives in.  The Killer Beetle grows into adulthood by incomplete metamorphosis.  It eats other insects and its predators are birds and bats.  The Killer Beetle defends itself by stabbing its sharp antenna like structures into its predators and injects a poison into it.
