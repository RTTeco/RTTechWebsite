Diptera

    These insects get there name Diptera because of there functional wings. The earliest know fossil flies are form about 150 million years ago. Many species of flies are attached to flowers and feed on nectar, some species prey on parasitize or  other insect species and animals. Some 120,000 species of flies are described as: presence of halters, modifications of the second pair of wings.

     The mouthparts of flies are also characteristically suctorial and many have large fleshy pads with drainage canals termed psedutracheae for efficient liquid uptake. Some flies have mouth modified for stabbing and piercing other insects, like the robber flies and dance flies. Mosquitoes and some other ectoparasitic groups have mouthparts modified for piercing hte skin of a vertebrate host and removing blood and other fluids.

    Diptera insects have a holometabolous life cycle. This means that the egg hatches into a small grub like creature which doesn't look anything like it is supposed to. The insect eats and grows and occasionally sheds its skin until it is big enough to make an adult fly.

     Recipes

Banana Worm Bread               

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup shortening
  • 3/4 cup sugar
  • 2 bananas, mashed
  • 2 cups flour
  • 1 teaspoon soda
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup chopped nuts
  • 2 eggs
  • 1/4 cup dry-roasted army worms

Directions:

Mix together all ingredients. Bake in greased loaf pan at 350 degrees for about 1 hour.

 

Crackers and Cheese Dip with Candied Crickets   

Ingredients:                                                                           

  • 8 oz. cream cheese
  • 4 oz. shredded cheddar cheese
  • 1 tsp. Worchestershire sauce
  • 2 tsp. chopped onions
  • 1 tsp. chopped green pepper
  • 2 tsp Miracle Whip®
  • candied crickets

Directions:

Soften cream cheese. Introduce remaining ingredients.

Spread mixture on cracker and top with a candied cricket.

 


 

    My bug is called the Blackachara. The Latin name for the Blackachara is Nigervose.  It is in the group called Diptera, and it is crossed with a bee and a fly. The Blackachara lives in trees. It can mainly be found in old squirrel holes. It lives in hot and humid climate areas, and are very harmful to birds. Blackachara can blend in with trees, because of its black and green color. The Blackachara grows into adulthood by molding. It will mold  three times before it is a full grow adult Blackachara. It molds every year in the fall. Blackacharas can live up to 8 years, at the most.

    The Blackachara uses its needle like sword to fight or kill off its predators. It eats fleas and other small insects. It will kill birds but it doesn't eat the birds, just kills them for fun. It predators are cat, which sometimes mistake them for birds, and do end up eating them form time to time.
