Siphonaptera

Fleas
Fleas are basically the only insects in the order Siphonaptera.  Fleas are wingless and bilaterally flat.  They have a piercing mouth part made for piercing the skin of an animal and sucking its blood.  Fleas have enlarged hind femurs used for jumping on to their hosts.  Fleas inhabit almost all climates in the world, except for the arctic climate zones.  As long as they have a host they are able to survive.  Fleas undergo a complete metamorphosis.  This means they go from an egg, to a larva, to a pupa, and completely mature into an adult flea.  Fleas are commonly found on mammals, lizards, and sometimes even birds.

Do Bug Zappers Really Work?

 According to studies done at Iowa State, no.  They concluded that they kill harmless insects that are vital to the aquatic food chain.  Other insects caught were biological control organisms.  Only .22% of the insects caught were harmful or biting insects.  Although I think they are wrong, and encourage people to us bug zappers.

Beetles in your flour?

 If you have beetles in your flour, you want to eliminate all potentially infected foods.  Then you want to clean the cupboards that contained the foods with a vacuum to suck up any hidden insects in cracks and corners.  To prevent reinfestation keep food enclosed in heavy plastic containers until you are sure all beetles are dead.

Meal Worm Fried Rice

 Ingredients:

1 egg, beaten

1 tsp. oil

3/4 c. water

1/4 c. chopped onions

4 tsp. soy sauce

1/8 tsp. garlic powder

1 c. minute rice

1 c. cooked mealworms

Directions:

Scramble egg in a saucepan, stirring to break egg into pieces.  Add water, soy sauce, garlic and onions.  Bring to a boil.  Stir in rice.  Cover; remove from heat and let stand five minutes.

Chocolate Covered Grasshoppers

 Ingredients:

baker's chocolate

candied crickets

Directions:

Melt baker's chocolate in double boiler.  Fill molds halfway with chocolate, add grasshoppers, fill rest of the way.  A tasty surprise in every one!

Here is an insect I have made up.  It has all of the adaptive features of any other Siphonaptera.

This is: Minuo Combibo

More commonly known as "Blood Sucker"

My insect belongs to the order Siphonaptera.  It's habitats include all climates except desert and arctic climates.  It has adapted itself to live in all other parts of the world.  It's adaptations include elongated hind femur's for jumping onto a host and a nose to help it locate its host.  It also has highly sensitive ears for detecting predators or a potential host.  My insect undergoes a complete metamorphosis to reach adult hood. It's prey include any warm blooded animals, they feed on the blood of these animals.  It has no natural predators. It defends itself with a neurotoxin in its circulatory system.  It is 500 times as toxic as a tarantula or black widow.  Very toxic!
