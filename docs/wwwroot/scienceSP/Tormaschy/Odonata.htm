Dragonflies and Damselflies

By Jason Tormaschy

Characteristics

Dragonflies like those in exisistance today were common on the earth 200 Million years ago.  Some characteristics of the odonatas are that the adult heads consist largely of compound eyes and their antennas are short and hair like.  There mouthparts are adapted for scooping and biting their prey.  There legs are used mainly to grasp a resting spot to help move them.  There legs are located far forward on the body. 

Development

Most of the dragonflies drop their eggs into the water or attach them to a stem of plants in the water.  Then the eggs develop into into nymphs and spend their lives entirely submerged only feeding on animals in the water.  Damselflies and Dragonflies undergo incomplete metamorphosis during their development.  A dragonfly may remain in nymphal from one to three or more years.  During that time, the nymph molts ten or more times.  Just before nymph become winged adults.

Importance and Behavior

Male dragonflies are known for their territorial behavior and will stake out an area to patrol as he continuously await for a passing female and will chase away any males from his area.  The male transfers sperm from his genitalia, on the end of the abdomen, to a special receptacle on his tail .  Then the female contacts the male receptacle with the end of her abdomen to receive the sperm.  They may remain in this looped position for some time and can even fly while hooked together.

Damselflies and Dragonflies are important in the food webs of freshwater lakes and streams.  The immature  forms are predators and live in freshwater.  The Green Darner is one of the most common dragonflies in the United States.  The Green Darner is a dragonfly with a brilliant  green head and thorax.

Why are beetles in flour and how to get rid of them? Beetles lay eggs in flour and other dry areas and stay dormant until there are proper conditions.  You can keep them out by having sealable glass, metal, or heavy plastic containers.  Another way is to put the container in the Refrigerator or freezer.

 

Banana Worm Bread

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup shortening
  • 3/4 cup sugar
  • 2 bananas, mashed
  • 2 cups flour
  • 1 teaspoon soda
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup chopped nuts
  • 2 eggs
  • 1/4 cup dry-roasted army worms

Directions: Mix together all ingredients. Bake in greased loaf pan at 350 degrees for about 1 hour.

 

Chocolate Chirpie Chip Cookies

Ingredients:

  • 2 1/4 cup flour
  • 1 tsp. baking soda
  • 1 tsp. salt
  • 1 cup butter, softened
  • 3/4 cup sugar
  • 3/4 cup brown sugar
  • 1 tsp. vanilla
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 12-ounce chocolate chips
  • 1 cup chopped nuts
  • 1/2 cup dry-roasted crickets

Directions: Preheat oven to 375 degrees. In small bowl, combine flour, baking soda and salt; set aside. In large bowl, combine butter, sugar, brown sugar and vanilla; beat until creamy. Beat in eggs. Gradually add flour mixture and insects, mix well. Stir in chocolate chips. Drop by rounded measuring teaspoonfuls onto un greased cookie sheet. Bake for 8-10 minutes.
