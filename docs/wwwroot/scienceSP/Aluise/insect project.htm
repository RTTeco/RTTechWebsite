Order Characteristics

    Homoptera is the order of insects that includes species such as cicadas, aphids, and leaf hoppers.  The species that one can learn about in this paragraph are cicadas.  Cicadas, commonly known as June bugs, are a relatively large insect.  Cicadas live in tropical or temperate regions, and feed on plants.  The adult lives for about 1 month before dying.  During mating, the cicada makes a loud buzzing sound to attract a mate. The fertilized egg is laid a few centimeters underground, and the nymph eats the sap from plant roots and may live underground for years before emerging as an adult.  Eventually the nymph grows wings and digs it's way out of the ground.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do Bug Zappers Work? Bug zappers do not work.  It is estimated that out of every 13,389 insects killed, only 31 are harmful.  That is about .22%.This means that the rest of the insects killed are either harmless or beneficial.  (Iowa State)
Beetles in Your Flour... If you get beetles in your flour, you can salvage it by freezing it for 1 week or heating it in a 140 degree oven for fifteen minutes.  Empty and thoroughly vacuum shelves.  Clean cupboards holding infested items paying particular attention to cracks and corners.  Empty vacuum cleaner or discard bag after use to prevent re-infestation.  (Iowa State)

Banana Worm Bread

Ingredients:

1/2 cup shortening
3/4 cup sugar
2 bananas, mashed
2 cups flour
1 teaspoon soda
1 teaspoon salt
1/2 cup chopped nuts
2 eggs
1/4 cup dry-roasted army worms

Directions:

Mix together all ingredients. Bake in greased loaf pan at 350 degrees for about 1 hour.             Iowa State

The Mirror Bug

    The insect pictured below is named Speculum Volaticus, or mirror bug.  The name comes from the wings, acting as a mirror on it's back, which can be used to confuse predators and to reflect light in order to temporarily blind the predator.  Some of these predators include birds, lizards, and snakes.  The wings can also be rotated 180 degrees in order to camouflage better and not glare.  The mirror bug is part of the order homoptera and is a close relative of the cicada.  It lives in almost every temperate or tropical area.  They are especially common in mountainous regions.  The adult eats plants while the nymph eats sap from plant roots.  The eggs are laid a few centimeters under ground, where the nymph hatches and lives for a few years.  Eventually the nymph will grow wings and emerge as an adult.  They live for about three months before they die having no other purpose than to reproduce.
