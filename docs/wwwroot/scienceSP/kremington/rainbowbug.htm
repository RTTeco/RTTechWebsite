   Lepidoptera  

Characteristics

By: Kori Remington

        Butterflies are a common animal with in the group of Lepidoptera. They are described as insects which have larvae that look nothing like the adults and having a complete metamorphosis. They also contain a pupa stage. They have two pairs of  wings that are membranous. Some female moths do not have them. Within the larvae mandibles are present but most often they are not in the adults. The antennae can be many different lengths. The wings and body of the adults are covered in scales and the body of the larva are generally covered in hairs. The salivary glands of the larvae have become modified to form the silk glands.

ANT TREATS  

Ingredients: 1 Pack of cinnamon twists

                      1 Pack of bendable straws

Directions: Use cinnamon twists as the insect body. Next insert the  bendable plastic straws in the side (three on each side) to be the legs. After you do this they should look like giant ants. You can use "donut holes" for ant eggs.

 Rainbow Bug

    Lepidoptera has many different kinds of butterflies within this class. They have wings and range in size. Antennas come from the top of their head. They help with their senses. They make life easier for the animal. The color for butterflies does not stay the same either. They vary from boring shades of brown to exotic shades of blue and yellow.   They have the ability to fly. This is a good advantage because it is an easy way to escape from other animals on land that would try to eat them. Some of the marks on the butterflies can be used as intimidation. They scare others away if they are on the bottom and they see them while the butterfly is in the air flying around.

 

The Rainbow bug has very unique marks. It used to be like a "normal" butterfly until one day it saw a pot of gold. While it was going for the pot of gold it flew through a rainbow. It never got the pot of gold, but it took on the colors of the rainbow and it has stayed with the bug forever. Red, blue, turquoise, and pink used to be the bugs favorite color so that is why the rainbow gave it extra of these colors. This butterfly appearing bug has four wings, two antennas, and loves skittles. Whenever it eats skittles it simply feels it can taste the rainbow! It will eat almost anything it can ,but prefers skittles. It lives mainly in the tropical areas of the world but occasionally you will find one in a colder climate.
