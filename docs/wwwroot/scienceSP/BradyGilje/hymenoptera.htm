Hymenoptera Characteristics

    Hymenoptera is the order that includes bees, ants, and other insect taxa collectively called wasps.  All hymenopterans have membrane wings.  One characteristic that all hymenopterans share is a constriction between the thorax and abdomen. 

   Many species of the class are social creatures, such as many bees and ants.  They have a highly developed social class made up of members divided into worker, drone, and queen.  These social insects live in hives or nests made of many thousands of individuals.  Some, however, are not social.  They live a solitary life and only come into contact with other individuals to mate.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do Bug Zappers really work? No, they do not work.  They do not kill very many of the targeted bugs (mosquitoes, gnats, etc...).  Moreover, they kill a lot of the harmless and beneficial bugs.
Explain beetles in your flour. That is talking about the red flour beetle which feeds on grains.  It can get inside your flour.  If that happens you should do one of the following:  throw the flour away, freeze it for one week, or heat it in the oven for 140 degrees for 15 minutes.

 

"Yummy" Insect Recipes

Chocolate Chirpie Chip Cookies

Ingredients:

·        2 1/4 cup flour

·        1 tsp. baking soda

·        1 tsp. salt

·        1 cup butter, softened

·        3/4 cup sugar

·        3/4 cup brown sugar

·        1 tsp. vanilla

·        2 eggs

·        1 12-ounce chocolate chips

·        1 cup chopped nuts

·        1/2 cup dry-roasted crickets

Directions:

Preheat oven to 375. In small bowl, combine flour, baking soda and salt; set aside. In large bowl, combine butter, sugar, brown sugar and vanilla; beat until creamy. Beat in eggs. Gradually add flour mixture and insects, mix well. Stir in chocolate chips. Drop by rounded measuring teaspoonfuls onto ungreased cookie sheet. Bake for 8-10 minutes.

Vespa carnis voro - "meat-eating wasp"

    The egg is where the bug hatches out of.  It comes out of the flat top part.  The smaller bug is the nymph, and the larger one is the adult.  The nymph and adult are exactly alike, except for size.  They stripes on the insect are used for protection.  They warn would-be predators that the bug is very poisonous and should not be eaten.  The wings are two pairs of membranous wings.  The legs are covered with small bristles that can detect vibrations.  On the mouth there are two large claws and rows of razor-sharp teeth, which allow the insect to eat meat and burrow inside of an animal.

      This bug is part of the order hymenoptera.  Unlike some other bees and wasps of the hymenoptera order, this bug is not social.  It lives a solitary life.  Individuals come together only for mating.  It lives in tropical areas of South America.  Its environment is very hot and wet.  To adapt to its environment it has gotten wings that still work when wet, and its exoskeleton prevents water gainage.  It also has a membrane underneath the exoskeleton that keeps the wasp cool.  Adults of the species are very poisonous.  Their venom in their sting is a very powerful neurotoxin. 

    These bugs have a short life cycle.  As soon as they leave the nest as adults they seek out a mate.  Once a male has mated, it dies.  When the female is done mating, it starts hunting.  These bugs usually eat small rodents.  The female finds a rodent and, using its stinger, injects its powerful neurotoxin.  The rodent dies instantly.  The bug proceeds to burrow into the rodent using its sharp two claws on the front of its mouth.  When inside, the insect eats for the first time as an adult.  It eats the meat inside the rodent using its razor-sharp teeth on its mouth.  After it has eaten its fill, its begins to lay its eggs inside the dead rodent.  After the egg is done laying the eggs, the female wasp then leaves the rodent and dies.  These bugs do not have a larva stage.  When they come out of the eggs, they are nymphs.  These nymphs eat on the meat in the rodent until they grow into adults.  Once they are adults, they leave their nest and go in search of a mate.  Since they don't eat until they are done mating, some of the wasps die of starvation before they find a mate.

    These wasps have no natural predators.  No other animal eats the bug because the venom of the wasp would kill any predators.  Its defense is the bright colors on its body which warn would-be predators that it is indeed poisonous.
