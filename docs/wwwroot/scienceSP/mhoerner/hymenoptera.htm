      Hymenoptera    

By: Michael Hoerner

Characteristics

    Hymenoptera contains more than 100,000 species of insects.  Some of these species have high complex social structures with some being parasitic.  They have mouthparts adapted for chewing and biting.  Most of them have two pairs of membranous wings, but some are wingless.  Some of them are very beneficial to humans with some being very destructive.  Ants, wasps, and bees are social insects with well-integrated colonies.

Frequently Asked Questions

Bug Zapper

 Bug zappers are very poor in killing mosquito females.  Black light traps are also useless for mosquitoes and are harmful to the environment.

Beetles in Flour

 Beetles in your flour originate in infested grain.  The adult beetle lays larvae in the food and then the adults wander away from the infested material.  You can freeze infested items for 1 week or heat at 140° for 15 minutes.  Empty and vacuum clean cupboards hold infested items.

 

Recipes

Banana Worm Bread

Ingredients

1/2 cup shortening
3/4 cup sugar
2 bananas, mashed
2 cups flour
1 teaspoon soda                                         
1 teaspoon salt
1/2 cup chopped nuts
2 eggs
1/4 cup dry-roasted army worms

Directions: Mix together all ingredients. Bake in greased loaf pan at 350 degrees for about 1 hour.

Chocolate Chirpie Chip Cookies

Ingredients:

2 1/4 cup flour
1 tsp. baking soda
1 tsp. salt
1 cup butter, softened
3/4 cup sugar
3/4 cup brown sugar
1 tsp. vanilla
2 eggs
1 12-ounce chocolate chips
1 cup chopped nuts
1/2 cup dry-roasted crickets

Directions: Preheat oven to 375. In small bowl, combine flour, baking soda and salt; set aside. In large bowl, combine butter, sugar, brown sugar and vanilla; beat until creamy. Beat in eggs. Gradually add flour mixture and insects, mix well. Stir in chocolate chips. Drop by rounded measuring teaspoonfuls onto ungreased cookie sheet. Bake for 8-10 minutes.

Giant Tiger Fly Vos Tigris Volucris

    The Giant Tiger Fly (Vos Tigris Volucris) ,part of the hymenoptera order, lives in tropical areas because it must keep moist to breathe.  It lives in the tropical forest where it eats nectar from the flowering trees there.  It has a extremely sensitive nose so it can smell nectar from certain plants that it likes.  It also has a somewhat hard skin so it helps to hold moisture inside itself so it doesn't become dehydrated.  It undergoes complete metamorphosis.  It has no real predators because it contains a poison in its hind end so that once it is broken open it releases the poison causing severe sickness.
