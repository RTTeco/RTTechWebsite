hemiptera

     Hemiptera means "half wing" and refers to the fact that part of the first pair of wings is  hard, while the rest of the first pair and the second pair are soft. Hemipterans also have modified piercing and sucking mouthparts; some suck plant juices and are plant pests, while others can bite painfully.

    A  group of insects known as the Homoptera is sometimes included within the Hemiptera, even though they lack the toughened areas on the first pair of wings. Some entomologists group both Hemiptera and Homoptera within the group Heteroptera; others use the name Heteroptera for what we have called the Hemiptera and use Hemiptera for the Heteroptera. The Homoptera have the most unique distinction of being probably the most destructive insects of all.

    Psyllids are small insects which suck plant juices. They damage plants by taking away the nutritional resources and infect plants with plant diseases. These bugs are becoming more of a problem in the US as new, exotic species are invading from other geographic regions of the world. It is important to be able to thoroughly identify newly invading species of these pests. Once identified, it is possible to determine where they came from. After the origin of these species is known, it is possible to collect biological control organisms from their native habitat and control them in this country. The ability to identify aphids can be difficult in some cases.

Crackers and Cheese Dip with Candied Crickets

Ingredients:

  • 8 oz. cream cheese

  • 4 oz. shredded cheddar cheese

  • 1 tsp. Worcestershire sauce

  • 2 tsp. chopped onions

  • 1 tsp. chopped green pepper

  • 2 tsp Miracle Whip®

  • candied crickets

Directions:

Soften cream cheese. Introduce remaining ingredients.

Spread mixture on cracker and top with a candied cricket.

Chocolate Chirpie Chip Cookies

Ingredients:

  • 2 1/4 cup flour

  • 1 tsp. baking soda

  • 1 tsp. salt

  • 1 cup butter, softened

  • 3/4 cup sugar

  • 3/4 cup brown sugar

  • 1 tsp. vanilla

  • 2 eggs

  • 1 12-ounce chocolate chips

  • 1 cup chopped nuts

  • 1/2 cup dry-roasted crickets

Directions:

Preheat oven to 375. In small bowl, combine flour, baking soda and salt; set aside. In large bowl, combine butter, sugar, brown sugar and vanilla; beat until creamy. Beat in eggs. Gradually add flour mixture and insects, mix well. Stir in chocolate chips. Drop by rounded measuring teaspoonfuls onto ungreased cookie sheet. Bake for 8-10 minutes.

 

The longus collium puteo volucris( long neck stink bug ) is a tropical insect, it lives mostly in the Amazon Rainforest. It's feeds on plants like ferns, and bushes. It lives in trees and logs. When it is in danger it lets out an unbearable smelling chemical that drives the predators away. It is called the long neck stink bug because of its distinctive long neck and the smell it gives off when it thinks it's in danger. This insect is part of the long necked order. The insect molts many times in his life before it becomes an adult. Its predators are birds and spiders.
