Coleoptera
By Andrea Hauck

 

Characteristics

he order Coleoptera includes many commonly encountered insects such as ladybird beetles, click beetles, scarabs, and fireflies.  They live throughout the world (except Antarctica), but are most specious in the tropics.  They range in size from as small as 0.3 mm long to well over 15 cm long.  They communicate to one another in many ways, by use of chemicals (pheromones), sounds, or by visual means.  They live in rainforests, deserts, lakes, and in the mountains.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do bug zappers really work? No, black light traps are not only useless for mosquitoes, they are harmful to the environment.  Pests, especially biting pests, do not end up in the traps.  They are very poor at killing mosquito females (the sex that bites).
What do you do if there are beetles in your flour? Beetles in your flour come from infested grain or from dry stored food products.  To get rid of the pests, you can try freezing the infested food for 1 week or heating in a 140 degree oven for 15 minutes.  Empty and thoroughly vacuum clean cupboards or shelves of infestation.

 

Recipes

Mealworm Fried Rice

Ingredients:
1 egg, beaten
1 tsp. oil
3/4 cup water
1/4 cup chopped onions
4 tsp. soy sauce
1/8 tsp. garlic powder
1 cup minute rice
1 cup cooked mealworm

Directions:
 Scramble egg in a saucepan, stirring to break egg into pieces.  Add water, soy sauce, garlic, and onions.  Bring to a boil.  Stir in rice.  Cover; remove from heat and let stand for five minutes.

Banana Worm Bread

Ingredients:
1/2 cup shortening
3/4 cup sugar
2 bananas, mashed
2 cups flour
1 tsp. soda
1 tsp. salt
1/2 cup chopped nuts
2 eggs
1/4 cup dry-roasted army worm

Directions:
Mix together all ingredients. Bake in greased loaf pan at 350 degrees for about 1 hour.

 

 

Cutzee Py Flutterby
(Pulchellus Seco)

y bug is called the Cutzee Py Flutterby.  Cutzee Py is because he is so cute.  Most animals don't eat him because he is too colorful.  His second name, Flutterby, is used because he looks so much like a butterfly.

e lives only in the Tropical Rainforests because that is where he is able to hide from predators so well.  His only predator is the Color Fly.  The Color Fly is jealous of the Flutterby's colorful body.  In the Rainforest, the colorful surroundings allow the Flutterby to blend in.  He is hard to see.  To protect itself, the Flutterby sprays a hot, irritating jet of gas at his attacker.  The spray is produced by mixing chemicals from two organs located in the thorax.

he Flutterby eats mostly leaves and berries.  His favorite food however is the Fluttering Butterball.  The Butterball is also very colorful.  That is why the Flutterby likes to eat him so much.  However, the Flutterby rarely can find the Butterball.  They only eat Butterballs a year.

or nesting, the Flutterby makes cuts in leaves, rolls them up, and lays eggs in the folds.  The life cycle of the Flutterby includes 4 parts.  The first part is the egg.  The eggs are laid in the leaf.  The eggs are oval-shaped and dull in color.  The second stage is the larva.  The larva are worm-like creatures.  The third stage is the pupa.  The pupa resembles the adult.  The last stage is the adult. 

he Flutterby is a unique creature.  Some say that he came about by the cross-breeding of a butterfly and a beetle.  Others, however, claim that he is all apart of your imagination.
