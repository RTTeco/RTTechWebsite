Isoptera

                               The most common species of termites is the subterranean termite.  They live in colonies beneath the soil.  Each colony consists of three groups of termites, which are the reproducers, workers, and soldiers.  In the colony, the king and the queen termites are the only active reproducers.  The queen will also lay one egg every 15 seconds.  Termites rarely expose themselves to light.  The worker termite has no eyes.  Its main function is to provide the colony with food.  The soldier termite has large jaws and its purpose is to defend the colony against enemies, such as ants.  Workers  need a high humidity to survive so they carry mud up to where they are feeding to maintain a 97 percent relative humidity.

                                Termites feed on wood and wood products that contain cellulose.  They have protozoa in their intestines which contain digestive enzymes that break down cellulose.  They have saw-toothed jaws that cut away wood one piece at a time.  Subterranean termites do not attack live trees.  Termites often infest buildings and lumber, wall paper, wood panels, sheetrock, and paper products.

                    imperium cavus plasmator (Power Driller)

            The Power Driller is a type of subterranean termite.  It gets its name from a long drill coming out from the front of the head that acts like a real power drill.  This allows the termite to construct tunnels through any kind of soil.  The drill is also used for a defense weapon.  The Power Driller has three sets of legs and two large mandibles that are used for chewing and cutting food.

 

                The Power Driller is from the order Isoptera.  Their habitat is moist and dark.  They like places with lots of wood.  The powerful drill enables the termite to drill holes through wood and soil .  The large mandibles chew and cut wood.  The antennas allow it to sense its environment in dark places.  The Power Driller goes through incomplete metamorphosis.  The Power Driller eats wood, paper products, and some plastics.  Its predators are ants and cockroaches.  The Power Driller defends itself with the drill on its head.  It can spin the drill up to 560 revolutions per minute which destroys its enemy from the inside out.
