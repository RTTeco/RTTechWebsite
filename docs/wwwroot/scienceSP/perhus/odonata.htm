Odonata
By Tasha Perhus

oth dragonflies and damselflies both belong to the order odonata.  As a nymph, the odonata have large eyes and antennas.  Their eyes contain 1/4 million facets.  The adult odonata also have very large eye and a pair of short antennas.  Their eyes have over 28,000 facets.  They have two sets of wings which allow them to fly very fast.  Their legs are weak and used to perch and hold prey.  They go through incomplete metamorphosis in which the larva lives under water for about two to three years.  During that time they molt between 20-30 times.  They have large functional mouthparts and feed on mosquitoes.

Chocolate Chirpie Chip Cookies

Ingredients:

2 1/4 cup flour
1 tsp. baking soda
1 tsp. salt
1 cup butter, softened
3/4 cup sugar
3/4 cup brown sugar
1 tsp. vanilla
2 eggs
1 12-ounce chocolate chips
1 cup chopped nuts
1/2 cup dry-roasted crickets

Directions:

Preheat oven to 375. In small bowl, combine flour, baking soda and salt; set aside. In large bowl, combine butter, sugar, brown sugar and vanilla; beat until creamy. Beat in eggs. Gradually add flour mixture and insects, mix well. Stir in chocolate chips. Drop by rounded measuring teaspoon.

Crackers and Cheese Dip with Candied Crickets

Ingredients:

8 oz. cream cheese
4 oz. shredded cheddar cheese
1 tsp. Worcestershire sauce
2 tsp. chopped onions
1 tsp. chopped green pepper
2 tsp Miracle Whip®
candied crickets

Directions:

Soften cream cheese. Introduce remaining ingredients. Spread mixture on cracker and top with a candied cricket.  Place on an ungreased cookie sheet. Bake for 8-10 minutes.

Color Fly
(coloratus Volucris)

he color fly has two sets of wings with colorful stripes.  It has four feet.  It has a blue tail and a yellow body

    he color fly as a part of the order Odonata.  The color fly lives in places that are warm and wet.  By flying very fast, the color fly releases excess heat.  The color fly grows into adult hood by incomplete metamorphosis.  It eats other animals that fly in the sky such as mosquitoes, moths, bees, and other color flies.  They can even eat some small birds  because they have very sharp teeth.
