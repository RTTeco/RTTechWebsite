Odonata

 

   Characteristics

 Dragon flies are from the order Odonata. Dragon flies are 1 to 4 inches in body length. Dragon flies are excellent flyers and can perform difficult aerial maneuvers when they are looking for food. Dragon flies mostly eat flies, mosquitoes, gnats and other fling insects. Dragon flies do not attack people.

    Dragon flies lay their eggs in or near water, and often attached to vegetation. The eggs are tossed into the water. The female may become submerged into the water during the egg laying process. Most dragon flies are nymphs longer then they are fling insects.

Recipes

Banana Worm Bread

Ingredients:

1/2 cup shortening
3/4 cup sugar
2 bananas, mashed
2 cups flour
1 teaspoon soda
1 teaspoon salt
1/2 cup chopped nuts
2 eggs
1/4 cup dry-roasted army worms

Directions:

Mix together all ingredients. Bake in greased loaf pan at 350 degrees for about 1 hour.

 

Chocolate Covered Grasshoppers

Ingredients:

baker's chocolate
candied crickets    

Directions:

Melt baker's chocolate in double boiler.

Fill molds halfway with chocolate, add grasshoppers, fill rest of the way.

Turbo Voluris (Helicopter Bug)

    The helicopter bug has huge eyes. Two large sets of wings for super flying. Two antennas for good reception. The helicopter bug has a long tail that is tapered at the end.

 The helicopter bug is in the order of the odonata. Its habitat is in moist places with warm temp. The helicopter bug has large eyes for seeing prey and large wings for fast flying. The helicopter bug grows to adult from incomplete metamorphosis. The helicopter bug eats harmful insects.  Its predators are fresh water fish when they are nymphs. The Helicopter bug has large wings fore a fast disappearance of predators.
