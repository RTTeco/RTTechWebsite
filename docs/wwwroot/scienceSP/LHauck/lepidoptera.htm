Lepidoptera  

 
By Lee Hauck
Lepidoptera is the order which moths and butterflies are in. There are148,000 different species of Lepidoptera. Some features of Lepidoptera are they have two pairs of large wings, compound-eyes, a proboscis, and an antennae. There are many different types of Butterflies. Butterflies have a threadlike antennae, brightly colored wings that play a role in mate recognition. Most butterflies fly during the day. Moth's antennaes are feathery. most moths are dull shades of brown. Males are attracted to  females by a powerful chemical signal that is released by the females. Most moths fly at night.

Chocolate Chirpie Chip Cookies

Ingredients:

2 1/4 cup flour
1 tsp. baking soda
1 tsp. salt
1 cup butter, softened
3/4 cup sugar
3/4 cup brown sugar
1 tsp. vanilla
2 eggs
1 12-ounce chocolate chips
1 cup chopped nuts
1/2 cup dry-roasted crickets

Directions:

Preheat oven to 375. In small bowl, combine flour, baking soda and salt; set aside. In large bowl, combine butter, sugar, brown sugar and vanilla; beat until creamy. Beat in eggs. Gradually add flour mixture and insects, mix well. Stir in chocolate chips. Drop by rounded measuring teaspoonfuls onto ungreased cookie sheet. Bake for 8-10 minutes.

www.chocolatechirpiechipcookies.com

Crackers and Cheese Dip with Candied Crickets

Ingredients:

8 oz. cream cheese
4 oz. shredded cheddar cheese
1 tsp. Worchestershire sauce
2 tsp. chopped onions
1 tsp. chopped green pepper
2 tsp Miracle Whip
candied crickets

Directions:

Soften cream cheese. Mix in remaining ingredients.

Spread mixture on cracker and top with a candied cricket.

Green Milkweed Drinker

The Green Milkweed Drinker is located in the tropical regions. The green Milkweed Drinker is from the Lepidoptera order. This is a butterfly that can camouflage its self like a tree, and can take the heat. It has a complete metamorphosis. It eats milkweed, and nectar. It has to look out for predators like birds, spiders, frogs, and lizards. Its color hides it from the predators. It also has dots on its wings that looks like eyes. The Green Milkweed Drinkers Latin name is Viridis Lac Lactis Comibibo.
