Recipes

Mealworm Fried Rice

Ingredients:

  •  1 egg, beaten

  • 1 tsp. oil

  • 3/4 c. water

  • 1/4 c. chopped onions

  • 4 tsp.  soy sauce

  • 1/8 tsp. garlic powder

  • 1 c. minute rice

  • 1 c. cooked mealworms

Directions:

Scramble egg in a saucepan, stirring to break egg into pieces.  Add water, soy sauce, garlic, and onions.  Bring to a boil.  Stir in rice.  Cover; remove from heat and let stand five minutes.

Chocolate Chirpy Chip Cookies

Ingredients:

  • 2 1/4 c. flour

  • 1 tsp. baking soda

  • 1 tsp. salt

  • 1 c. butter, softened

  • 3/4 c. sugar

  • 3/4 c. brown sugar

  • 1 tsp. vanilla

  • 2 eggs

  • 1 12- oz chocolate chips

  • 1 c. chopped nuts

  • 1/2 c. dry-roasted crickets

Directions:

Preheat oven to 375 degrees. In a small bowl, combine flour, baking soda, and salt; set aside.  In a large bowl, combine butter, sugars, and vanilla; beat until creamy.  Beat in the eggs.  Gradually add flour mixture and insects, mix well.  Stir in chocolate chips.  Drop by rounded measuring teaspoonfuls onto ungreased cookie sheet. Bake for 8-10 minutes.

color fly

(Coloratus Tractus Volucris)

 

    This unique and rare insect from the order  of Odonata is found in Mexico, and has an highly adaptive tolerability  for  immense heat.

    Unlike his other species in his order, this type of Odonata live in colonies and live a somewhat "civil" lifestyle, which is what allows this species to be so advanced.

    The color fly has the gained the protection advantages against his predators by having horns and extremely strong legs for wrestling its prey, the horns are razor sharp at the ends to instantaneously kill it's prey.  Also, their long strong legs provide support for it when it sleeps, due to the fact that it sleeps standing up. Losing, the long tail from the dragon fly this then allows the color fly to hide in smaller areas, but has a disadvantages because of its brightly colored body. The color fly has gained the larger wingspread of 3 in. for a faster flight, which also aids in a fast escape from predators.

    This insect feeds on small house flies, fire flies, fleas, and gnats, and it's predators are smaller birds, such as sparrows, butterflies, and bug zappers (no kidding!).

    The growth stages of the color fly species is an incomplete metamorphosis, which means they start out in an egg of some sort, somewhere, and then enter the nymph stage before becoming a full grown adult. In this species' case it stays there for about 2 years before adulthood.
