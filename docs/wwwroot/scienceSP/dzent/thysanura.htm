  Thysanura
                                                                 By: DelRae Zent                                                                       

    Silverfish are fast-running insects that hide under stones or leaves during the day and emerge at night to search for food.  A few species are resistant to despoiling and are well adapted to survive in domestic environments such as basements and attics. Silver fish are scavengers; they survive on a wide range of food, but seem to prefer a diet of algae, lichens, or starchy vegetable matter.

    Thysanurans may live from three to eight years.  They continue to molt frequently, even after reaching adulthood.

Banana Worm Bread

Ingredients:

1/2 cup shortening
3/4 cup sugar
2 bananas, mashed
2 cups flour
1 teaspoon soda
1 teaspoon salt
1/2 cup chopped nuts
2 eggs
1/4 cup dry-roasted army worms

Directions:

Mix together all ingredients. Bake in greased loaf pan at 350 degrees for about 1 hour.

Chocolate Chirpie Chip Cookies

Ingredients:                                                              

2 1/4 cup flour
1 tsp. baking soda
1 tsp. salt
1 cup butter, softened
3/4 cup sugar
3/4 cup brown sugar
1 tsp. vanilla
2 eggs
1 12-ounce chocolate chips
1 cup chopped nuts
1/2 cup dry-roasted crickets

Directions:

Preheat oven to 375. In small bowl, combine flour, baking soda and salt; set aside. In large bowl, combine butter, sugar, brown sugar and vanilla; beat until creamy. Beat in eggs. Gradually add flour mixture and insects, mix well. Stir in chocolate chips. Drop by rounded measuring teaspoonfuls onto ungreased cookie sheet. Bake for 8-10 minutes.

Long Fish

(Longus Piscis)

The long fish has antennas that allow it to sense direction.  Its bright colors scare predators away.  The long fish has six legs that allow it to move across the ground.

                            

The long fish is a part of the order Thysanura.  It has many of the same characteristics as a silver fish.  They spend most of their live concealed  beneath or within stones, wood, houses, and the soil.  Thysanura mostly feed on dead plant material and fungal hyphae.  There are more than 720 species in the world, nine of which have been found in the British Isles.
