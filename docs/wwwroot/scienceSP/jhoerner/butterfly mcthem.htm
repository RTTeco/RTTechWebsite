HIPPIE BUTTERFLY

    The main characteristics of the Order Lepidoptera are antennas, compound eyes, three pairs of legs, a hard exoskeleton, wings covered by scales, and the outer body covered by sensory hairs.  All butterflies have three body parts: the head, thorax, and the abdomen.  The head region carries the sensory apparatus.  The thorax region contains the legs and muscles, and houses the large muscles that control the wings and legs.  The abdomen area contains digestive, excretory, and reproductive organs.  The life span of a butterfly is approximately 3 to 6 weeks.  Butterfly larvae have chewing mouth parts to eat various plant material, while adult butterflies have siphoning parts to suck nectar from plants, flowers, or fruits.  A butterfly habitat must have sunlight, plants and flowers, plants on which to lay eggs, water, and basking or roosting areas.  To attract mates, female butterflies release pheromones, perfume, into the air.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

Do Bug Zappers Work? From what you may think, bug zappers do not work at killing insect pests, the ones that bite.  Instead, bug zappers kill harmless aquatic insects or predators that keep the insect pest population down. Information available at Iowa State.
How do you get rid of beetles in your flour? To get rid of beetles in your flour, you must locate and eliminate all possible infested food items.  Then, you should empty and clean all cupboards holding infested food items.  Use a vacuum cleaner.  Insecticide sprays are not recommended.   Information at Iowa State.

YUMMY RECIPES

Crackers and Cheese Dip with Candied Crickets

Ingredients: 8 oz of cream cheese, 4 oz of Shredded cheddar cheese, 1 tsp of Worchester sauce, 2 tsp of chopped onions, 1 tsp of chopped green pepper, 2 tsp. of Miracle Whip, and last, your Candied Crickets.

Directions:  Soften the cream cheese.  Introduce your ingredients.  Spread the mixture onto cracker and top with a candied cricket. Information at Iowa State.
Chocolate Chirpie Chip Cookies

Ingredients: 2 1/4 cup flour, 1 tsp. baking soda, 1 tsp salt, 1 cup softened butter, 3/4 cup sugar, 1 tsp vanilla, 2 eggs, 1 12-oz chocolate chips, 1 cup chopped nuts, and 1/2 cup dry-roasted crickets.

Directions: Preheat oven to 375.  In a small bowl, combine flour, baking soda and salt, set aside.  In large bowl, combine butter, sugar, brown sugar, and vanilla; beat until creamy.  Beat in eggs.  Gradually add flour mixture and insects, mix well.  Stir in chocolate chips.  Drop by rounded measuring teaspoonfuls onto an ungreased cookie sheet.  Bake for 8-10 minutes.  Information at Iowa State.

Rebellis Adulatio Volucris

Hippie Butterfly

    The Hippie Butterfly comes from the order of insects Lepidoptera.  The Hippie Butterfly's habitat consists of plentiful sun, plants for food and on which to lay eggs, and basking areas.  The Hippie Butterfly is found only at the bottom of the Himalayan Mountains.  The larvae, caterpillar form, of this butterfly has adapted mouth parts strong enough to eat evergreen needles.  The adult butterfly has siphoning tubes to suck nectar from plants and flowers.  The Hippie Butterfly's main predators are bats, birds, and spiders.  As defense, the Hippie Butterfly has black and red wings that tell its predators that it is poisonous so they do not eat it.
