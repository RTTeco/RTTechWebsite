Lepidoptera  

Characteristics

By: Kori Remington

        Butterflies are a common animal with in the group of Lepidoptera. They are described as insects which have larvae that look nothing like the adults and having a complete metamorphosis. They also contain a pupa stage. They have two pairs of  wings that are membranous. Some female moths do not have them. Within the larvae mandibles are present but most often they are not in the adults. The antennae can be many different lengths. The wings and body of the adults are covered in scales and the body of the larva are generally covered in hairs. The salivary glands of the larvae have become modified to form the silk glands.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do bug Zappers really work? No they do not really work. The do not kill the harmful bugs. The females bite and they kill more males. They also kill harmless bugs.
How can you prevent beetles from going in your flour? You can prevent beetles from going into your flour by coming and cleaning out your cupboards or shelves holding invested items. The red flour beetle can be found in flour and dried grain. If you keep your food area clean you can prevent them.
