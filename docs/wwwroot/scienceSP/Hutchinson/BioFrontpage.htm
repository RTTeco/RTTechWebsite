Neuroptera

 

Characteristics

Neuroptera are a diverse and very interesting group of about 5400 species of insects which seem so often to miss out on the attention they deserve.  Neuroptera include lacewings, Dobson flies, Mantid flies, Alderflies, snake flies, Sponge flies, Ant lions, and Owl flies.  They are described as soft-bodied insects of a variable size usually with a long antennae.  They have biting and chewing mouthparts both as larvae and as adults, though some of the adults do not feed.  Generally they have 2 pairs of wings, the hind pair are usually considerably bigger.  Neuroptera have ten segments to their abdomen and 5 to their tarsi.  They also have  large compound eyes.

Planipennia

Planipennia include green lacewings, brown lacewings, Ant lions, Sponge flies, Mantid flies, and Owl flies. Green lacewings are by far the most common of the the insects in the class neuroptera.  The larvae of lacewings are all terrestrial.  They have their ears in the large veins of their wings which allow them to detect the sonic of bats, their main predators.  The eggs of green lacewings are laid on the end  of a thin stalk.

Megaloptera

Megaloptera include Alderflies, Dobsonflies, and snakeflies.  The 300 odd species of Megaloptera are distinguished by the large pleated fan-like section on the hind wing of the adults.  The larvae of Dobsonflies and Alderflies are all aquatic, however they leave the water to pupate.  The pupae have functioning legs and mandibles and can walk as well as bite in self defense.  Adult Dobsonflies can be as large as 3-4 inches in length.  The males have very large mandibles which the males use to fight each other. As adults Dobsonflies and Alderflies do not feed.

Mantidflies or mantispids possess raptorial forelegs much like those of the similarly named praying mantids which they use in the same way.  There are about 400 species of Mantidflies.  Like the Planipennia all their larvae are terrestrial.  The males use pheromones instead of sound to attract females.  The eggs, like those of lacewings are laid at the end of a thin stalk.  The eggs soon hatch and immediately enter a state of diapause.  In the spring they seek out an eggsac of a lycosid spider which it enters.

Chocolate Chirpie Chip Cookies

Ingredients:

  • 2 1/4 cup flour
  • 1 tsp. baking soda
  • 1 tsp. salt
  • 1 cup butter, softened
  • 3/4 cup sugar
  • 3/4 cup brown sugar
  • 1 tsp. vanilla
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 12-ounce chocolate chips
  • 1 cup chopped nuts
  • 1/2 cup dry-roasted crickets

Directions:

Preheat oven to 375. In small bowl, combine flour, baking soda and salt; set aside. In large bowl, combine butter, sugar, brown sugar and vanilla; beat until creamy. Beat in eggs. Gradually add flour mixture and insects, mix well. Stir in chocolate chips. Drop by rounded measuring teaspoonfuls onto ungreased cookie sheet. Bake for 8-10 minutes.

The common name of my insect is the lion fly and its scientific name is leo volucris.  He is in the order neuroptera.  The lion fly lives in moist warm areas near water.  It's large wings are adaptive because they fly around to the top of trees to eat bud off the trees.  The insect is born in the larvae stage and takes 5-10 days to mature into adulthood.  They eat most green plants in their environment including leaves and tree buds.  Its predators are the wasp and the dragonfly. It defend itself by biting the predator and releasing a venom through its mouth that can kill other smaller insects.
