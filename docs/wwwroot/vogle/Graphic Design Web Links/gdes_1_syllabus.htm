Course Overview
  This course is designed for creation of projects to be used by the school and individuals; the study of career possibilities in colleges and art schools using conditions as nearly like a commercial studio as possible, i.e., deadlines, rough drafts, conferences with client, layouts lettering, completed work critiques, delivery, and billing. Learning the technology program Photoshop will be included in the processes of creative rendering.

Course Objectives
 

Learn basic graphic design theory and be able to practice the art of design through a variety of layout techniques.
Learn through classroom participation and discussion.
Learn how to have layout completed into print, and on time using the PC computer. 
Software to know will be Adobe Photoshop
Student Learning Outcomes
 

1.  To develop higher order and critical thinking skills.
2.  To develop basic academic success skills including proficiencies in writing and the   
     use of technology.
3.  To develop discipline specific knowledge and skills within each major.
4.  To develop liberal arts and academic values and knowledge.
5.  To develop work and career preparation skills.
6.  To develop the personal development of each student in order to maximize this potential for      productive careers in a life-long learning model.
Teaching Strategies
 

1. Content of the course will be provided through lectures, demonstrations, group research, assigned readings, and practical assignments.  Students are encouraged to participate in classroom discussions and to ask for clarification or greater detail.
2. In most cases examples will be used to supplement discussions on various topics especially those related to visual design. 
3. Students are encouraged to contact the instructor directly, either during or after class, to discuss issues or content which cause concern.
4. Written assignments are used to encourage the student to seek information to supplement course material and to allow him/her to investigate aspects of the discipline, which have more of a personal interest.
5. Quizzes and Tests are administrated to ensure comprehension.
