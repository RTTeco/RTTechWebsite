Political Art
Rulers commission works of art designed to project their political image and enhance their power. 
Today we are familiar with George Washington’s image on the U.S. $1.00 bill and .25 coin.  The very fact that Washington is represented on one side of the $1 bill reflects his role as the first—the number 1– president of the United States.  On the other side of the bill, the large “ONE” is flanked by the obverse and reverse of the the Great Seal, which was designed in 1782 but placed on the $1 bill only in 1935.
