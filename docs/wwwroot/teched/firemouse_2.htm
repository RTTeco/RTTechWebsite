THE FIREMOUSE TWO

Objective: To learn the difference between potential and kinetic energy. Work independently or cooperatively. Work with only assigned materials. Work within a given limit of time.

Problem: Given the approved materials, extinguish a standard birthday candle, pop a air filled water balloon, and raise a flag beginning with the potential and kinetic energy of a mousetrap. This problem will be accomplished in not less than 20 seconds, and no more than 2 minutes.

Limits: One day of planning (homework).
Work in groups of no more than two.
Each group will have an allotted time limit to set up.
Each group will have as many attempts as time allows or candle allows.
The candle must remain standing after it is extinguished.
Any one item within the Firemouse Two cannot have a value of over $2.00
The contents of the firemouse or material used must be safe (Instructors discretion)
The Firemouse must be mobile

Given: One base plate approximately 20.32cm x 111.76cm
One standard mousetrap
One birthday candle
One flag on a staff
One water balloon
The set up: Mousetrap and candle are placed on base plate.
A hole may be drilled to fit the candle.

Evaluation: A-The flame is extinguished, the flag is raised and the balloon popped after the first attempt in the allotted time frame

B-The flame is extinguished, the flag is raised and the balloon popped after the second or third attempt in the allotted time frame

C-The flame is extinguished, the flag is raised and the balloon popped after the fourth or fifth attempt in the allotted time frame.

D- The flame is extinguished before the candle burns itself out.

F- No Firemouse Two submitted.

Bonus: Bonus points will be given for each energy transfer. Subjective judgment on uniqueness of designs and material used the instructor will award 1-10 points for creative solutions. This score will be added to the actual performance of the Firemouse Two to determine the final score.
