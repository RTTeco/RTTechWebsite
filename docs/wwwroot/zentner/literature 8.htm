In this area of study, the students read various forms of literature for meanings of the various elements of each form.  These forms include short stories, poems, essays, novellas, and novels.

Each section of the literature series has a title which explains the theme of each section.  The following is a list of sections studied in Eighth Grade English:

The Adventures of Tom Sawyer - We begin literature by reading this fun novel about a young boy who continuously gets himself and others into trouble.

The Fighting Dragons unit explores the various "dragons" life holds for various characters.  Starting with the original fire-breathing dragon, this section talks of different sorts of dragons including misunderstandings and differences in people.  The section culminates with the epic poem "Beowulf".

Winning Out is a section that the students can really relate to. It involves characters that have to overcome obstacles of some sort to win whether that obstacle is gender, acceptance, or misunderstanding of people.  This section end with either one of two popular novels, The Incredible Journey or Holes.

The Survival unit shows how characters survive against unthinkable odds.  Some are successful and others pay a price.  My Brother Sam is Dead is the novel that ends this unit.
