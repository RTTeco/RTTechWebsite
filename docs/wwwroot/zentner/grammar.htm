In this area of study, students get an understanding of the concepts of grammar and the correct usage of all aspects of grammar.

The following are the areas of study in which the students are instructed:

Subjects, Predicates, and Sentences - In this area the students get an understanding of the structure of a sentence; simple, complete, and compound subjects and predicates; kinds of sentences; and sentence fragments and run-ons.

Nouns - This section explains the kinds of nouns our language encompasses including common, proper, concrete, abstract, compound, possessive, plural, collective, and appositives.

Verbs - All the types of verbs are explained in this section.  They include action, transitive, intransitive, linking, and helping verbs.  This section also explains direct objects, indirect objects, various verb forms, and verb tenses.

Pronouns - Pronouns are the words that take the place of nouns.  The areas of study in this section includes personal, possessive, indefinite, reflexive, intensive, and interrogative.  This section also explains what an antecedent is and how the pronoun renames it.

Adjectives and Adverbs - Both of these are modifiers.  In this section, the students get a better understanding of what these modifiers are and how they work.  the areas of study in this section includes adjectives, articles, comparative and superlative adjectives, demonstratives, adverbs, intensifiers, comparative and superlative adverbs, and avoiding double negatives.

Prepositions, Conjunctions, and Interjections - Prepositional phrases, objects of the preposition, how these phrases are used in the sentence, conjunctions, and interjections are all covered in this section.  The students will be able to tell what every word in a sentence is after this section is completed.

Clauses - In this section, the students will learn the purpose of clauses and how they are used in sentences.  A new type of sentence in introduced in this section: the complex sentence.  The clauses discussed in this section include clauses that act as adjectives, adverbs, and nouns.

Phrases - The phrases introduced in this section are all verbals; they include participles, gerunds, and infinitives. 

Subject-Verb Agreement - Problems with agreements between subjects and verbs are discussed in this section.  Problems arise when there is usage of collective nouns, indefinite pronouns, and compound subjects.

Diagramming - The purpose of diagramming is to explain the usage of each of the parts of speech and where they fit and don't fit.  After a close study of this, the students should obtain a better understanding of the usage of all parts of speech thus transferring the knowledge to their spoken and written language.

Capitalization - In this section, students get the rules of proper capitalization.

Punctuation - The use of commas, semicolons, colons, quotation marks, and end marks are all explained in this grammar section.
