4.  Attendance/Participation:

                        You are expected to attend and participate in class on a daily basis. Students will receive a weekly participation grade based on class attendance and activity in the classroom.  Participation is asking question, working as an individual, or in a group setting. 

 

5.  Computer Lab Procedures and Behavior: 

                        A.  Students are expected to come to class prepared with books, notebooks pencils and other materials needed to function in computer class.  There will be no running back and forth to lockers during class.  Once in class you are to take your seat and wait for instructions from the teacher as to what task we will doing that day.  You are not to logon to the computer until told to do so.  There are no games, music, e-bay or other Internet surfing privileges unless it is part of an assignment from a teacher.  Violation of this rule will result in:

 

1.      Be warned of the misconduct.

2.      Loss of computer privileges for that day.

3.      Loss of computer privileges for a time determined by the principal and teacher. ( May be a week , month, or entire year)

 

                       

 

                        B.  Students will demonstrate appropriate behavior in the classroom.  Students who exhibit inappropriate behavior will:

 

1.      Be warned of the misconduct.

2.      Be sent out of the room.

                                    3.   Be sent to the Principal’s office.          

 

6.  Assignments:

                        Assignments are due on the date assigned. All assignments must be saved on the server in your student folder.  Do not delete files in your folder until you have received the grade.  Record all grades in your planner.  All assignments are placed in the INBOX, and all tests are turned into the teacher.  On all assignments, tests and papers your name must appear.  The document should have a professional, business like appearance.  There are to be no folds or wrinkles. Watch for correct spelling and grammar.  If you have more than 5 errors it will be returned with a point deduction and you will get one chance to correct it.  If it is handed in the second time with still 5 or more errors it will be graded at 50% of the original grade.
