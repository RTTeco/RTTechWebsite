  • With the Czar gone, Alexandra made the important decisions after consulting with Rasputin
  • Russian people did not like the Czar in power, wanted change
      • Dec 1916 assassinated Rasputin
      • Shot 3 times
      • tied up, threw in river, drowned but got knots out

March Revolution
  • March 1917  women in Petrograd started protests which lead to strikes.
    • Bread rationing, worked 12 hours then stood in line for bread, got none
    • Mar. 8  10,000 women marched
      • Other workers joined in
    • Mar. 10  strike shut down all the factories in the city
    • Nicholas ordered troops to fire on the crowd
    • Many soldiers were part of the strikers
42
March Revolution
  • Duma- legislative body in 1917


  • Nicholas wanted the Duma dissolved


  • Duma meet and called for an end to the Czars


  • March 15  Nicholas reluctantly stepped down
    • Ended the 300 yr old Romanov dynasty & Czar ruling class

Provision Govt
  • Aleksandr Kerensky  head of the provisional govt. He decided to keep Russia in WWI
  • Soviets  representative councils of workers, soldiers & peasants


  • Soviets wanted more changes to the govt
    • Made up of more radical interests
    • Bolsheviks  small faction of the Russian Social Democrat Party

From Czar to Communists
  • Vladimir Lenin  became the leader of the Bolsheviks, did not like capitalism & democracy
    • Became a party of violent revolution
  • 1900-1917  Lenin spent most of his time abroad
  • Germans saw an opportunity to cause chaos, shipped Lenin home to Russia
  • Lenins arrival opened a new stage of the Russian Revolution
45
Lenin
  • Wanted to use the
  • Soviet group to overthrow the provisional govt
  • He promised
    • End to the war
    • Redistribute all the land to peasants
    • Transfer factories & industries from capitalists to committees of workers
    • Transfer power of govt to the Soviets
46
Bolsheviks Seize Power
  • Oct 1917  Bolsheviks grew from 50,000 to 240,000
  • They were in a position to claim power in the name of the Soviets
  • Nov 6, 1917  Bolshevik forces seized the Winter Palace (seat of the provisional govt)
  • Publicly Lenin turned the power over to the Congress of Soviets
    • The real power passed to a council headed by Lenin
    • Bolsheviks renamed themselves the Communists
47
Bolsheviks Seize Power

    • Gained temporary peace
    • Russia soon entered a civil war.

Civil War in Russia
  • Many people were opposed to the new communist govt
    • Liberals & others loyal to the Czar, expected a more democratic system, not a dictator like Lenin
    • Joined by the Allies, concerned about Communism
  • Allies sent thousands of troops & supplies to Russia
    • Tried to get Russia back into WWI
    • Red Army = Communists
    • White Army = anti-communists

Civil War in Russia

    • Took control of neighbors :
      • Georgia, Armenia, Azerbaijan, retook the Ukraine
  • Royal Family
    • April 1918 move to the Ural mountains
    • July 16 local soviets murdered the Czar, his wife & five kids
    • Burned their bodies in a mine shaft

  • Leon Trotsky  Red Armys commissar
    • Soldiers who deserted or disobeyed were executed on the spot
  • War communism  policy ensuring supplies for the Red Army

New German Attack
  • March 1918  Start of German attack
  • April 1918  Germans were 50 miles from Paris
  • July 1918  Second Battle of the Marne

  • Germans found that the Allies were unwilling to make peace with the current govt
  • Nov 9, 1918  Emperor William II gave in to public pressure and left the country
  • Germany set up a democratic republic type of govt
  • Armistice  truce agreement
  • Nov 11, 1918  Germany signs an armistice with the allies
58
Peace Settlements
  • January 1919
    • representative of the 27 victorious Allied nations met in Paris to make a final settlement of World War I
59
Wilsons Proposals
  • Wilsons Fourteen Points
    • Reaching peace agreements openly rather than through secret diplomacy
    • Reducing armaments to a point consistent with domestic safety
    • Ensure self-determination
      • (right of each people to have their own nation)
  • Wilson became the leader for new world order based on democracy & cooperation
  • When got to the conference was met with other ideas
60
Paris Peace Conference
  • Delegates met in Paris early in 1919
  • National interests complicated the discussions
    • Great Britain
      • David Lloyd George  Prime Minister
      • Make the Germans pay for the war
    • France
      • Georges Clemenceau  Premier of France
      • Desired revenge & security against future German attacks
        • Reparations  payments to cover war costs
        • Wanted Germany stripped of all weapons
        • Wanted a separate Rhineland as a buffer between France & Germany
61
Paris Peace Conference
  • The Big Three
    • US  Wilson
    • Britain  Lloyd George
    • France  Clemenceau
    • Made all the big decisions
      • Wilson wanted a world organization The League of Nations to prevent future wars
      • Britain & France wanted to punish Germany
62
Paris Conference Compromise
  • Compromise
    • Accepted the idea of the League of Nations
    • Wilson agreed to territorial arrangements, believed these could be fixed later by the League
    • France gave up the buffer area and accepted a defensive alliance with the US & GB.
      • US Senate refused to ratify it
63
Final Treaties
  • Final peace settlements of the Paris Conference consisted of 5 separate treaties with:
    • Austria, Hungary, Bulgaria, Turkey
  • Treaty of Versailles  signed with Germany & was the most important treaty
  • Germans felt it was a very harsh peace settlement
    • Angered by the dictated peace  but accepted it anyway
64
Treaty of Versailles
  • Conditions of the Treaty
    • Article 231  War guilt clause, Germany had to pay reparations for all damages caused by the war
    • Reduce their army to 100,000 men, Cut back the navy & Eliminate the air force
    • Give back to France territories of Alsace & Lorraine
    • Parts of east Germany were made into Poland
    • Land along the Rhine river became a demilitarized zone, buffered France
65
Legacies of the War
  • Redrew the map of Europe
    • Germany, Russia, Aust-Hung lost the most
    • New nations were formed
      • Finland, Latvia, Estonia, Lithuania, Poland, Czechoslovakia, Austria, Hungary, Romania, Yugoslavia (Serbs)
  • Left nations with ethnic minorities, lead to later conflicts
  • Allies promised the Arabs independent nations for fighting against the Ottomans
    • After the war, France & Britain took control of some of these areas
