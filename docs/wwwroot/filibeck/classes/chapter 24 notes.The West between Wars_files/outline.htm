French Demands
  • April 1921 – Allied Reparations Commission
    • Determined that Germany owed 132 billion German Marks
    •   (33 billion US dollars)
    • Payable in annual payments of 2.5 billion marks
    • Germany made the 1st payment in 1921
    • Germany went into financial crisis & couldn’t pay any more
    • France sent troops to occupy the Ruhr Valley which was Germany’s industrial & mining center
7
Inflation in Germany
  • Germans did not fight the French, but workers strike
  • Germans paid workers by printing more money
  • Inflation – rise in prices
  • Inflation of the German Mark
    • 1914 – 4.2 marks = 1 US dollar
    • Nov 1, 1923 – 130 billion marks = 1 US dollar
    • Nov 30, 1923 – 4.2 trillion marks = 1 US dollar
  • Germany & France wanted a solution to the money problem
8
Dawes Plan
  • Aug 1924 – International Commission
    • Chairman was Charles Dawes, an American banker
      • Reduced the amount of reparations
      • Coordinate Germany’s annual payments with its ability to pay
      • Granted a $200 million loan to Germany for recovery


  • American businesses started investing in European business
  • 1924-1929 – period of European prosperity

Great Depression

  • Causes of the Depression – 3 factors
    • Series of downturns in the economies of many nations in the late 1920’s
    • International financial crisis
    • US Stock market crash of 1929

Fascism in Italy
  • Mussolini take over


    • 1922 – Mussolini & the Fascist threaten to march on Rome
    • King made Mussolini the new Prime Minister
    • Set up new policies
      • Made himself the head of gov’t with the power to make new laws
      • Favored only the Catholic Church
      • Police were given unrestricted authority to arrest any opposition
      • Created a secret police

Fascism in Italy
  • Used propaganda to influence the people
    • Censored all mass media like newspapers, radio, movies
    • Wanted to mold the Italians into a single-minded group
  • Wanted to create a nation of new Italians who were fit, disciplined and war loving
  • Mussolini did compromise with Italy’s traditional institutions like the church
    • Recognized Vatican City as its own nation

New era in Russia
  • War communism hoarded food & supplies, people grew unhappy
  • New Economic Policy (NEP) – new policy by Lenin, modified version of the old capitalist system
    • Peasants could produce openly
    • Retail stores could be privately owned
  • 1922 Lenin & the Communists created a new state called the Union of Soviet Socialist Republics (USSR)
  • NEP saved the USSR from total economic disaster
22
USSR Industrialism
  • 1924 – Lenin died
  • Politburo – leading policy-making committee in the USSR
  • Struggle for power in the politburo for control of the country
  • 2 groups divided the politburo
    • Group 1 - Led by Trotsky
      • Wanted to end NEP, have rapid industrialization, spread communism
    • Group 2 –
      • Did not want to spread communism & focus on building a strong socialist state in Soviet Union

Rise of Stalin
  • Joseph Stalin


    • Held a gov’t job of Party General Secretary
    • Appointed many regional, district and local leaders
    • Wanted complete control of the Communist party
    • Gained support from the thousands of officials he appointed
    • By 1929 – Stalin had established a powerful dictatorship
24
Stalin in Control
  • Beginning of his era had a more sweeping effect than the revolution of 1917.
  • Five-Year Plans – set economic goals for five-years
    • Purpose was to transform the USSR from an agricultural into an industrial country overnight
      • Quadrupled the production of heavy machines
      • Doubled oil production

Costs of Industry

  • Families lived in miserable conditions
  • Strict laws limited where people could live
  • Propaganda stressed the need to sacrifice for the gov’t
  • Collectivization – elimination of private farms by Stalin, gov’t owned all the land & the peasants who worked on it

Stalin’s Control
  • Collectivization was completed at a high cost
    • Peasants hoarded food & killed livestock – led to famine
    • 10 million peasants died from 1932-33
  • Stalin strengthened his control over the party
    • Resistance was sent to labor camps in Siberia
    • Put on trial then to death all the old Bolshevik leaders
    • Purged army officers, diplomats, union officials, educators, ordinary citizens.
      • 8 million were arrested
        • many sent to Siberia & never returned
        • Many were simply executed

Rise of Hitler
  • Adolf Hitler – born April 20, 1889 in Austria
    • He was unsuccessful in school
    • Traveled to Vienna to be an artist, rejected by the academy
      • Here he developed his political ideas
      • Realized political parties could use propaganda & terror
      • Spent 4 years on the Western Front in WWI
    • 1919 – joined the German Workers party
      • 1921 – took total control of the party & renamed National Socialist German Workers Party (NSDAP) or Nazi for short
      • 1923 – added a militia, known as SA or Storm Troops or Brownshirts

Rise of Hitler

  • Quickly crushed, Hitler was sentenced to prison
  • While in prison he finalize his political theories
    • Mein Kampf – book wrote by Hitler explaining his philosophy
    • His political theory
      • Racism, especially anti-Semitism (hostility towards Jews)
      • Nationalism
      • Darwinian social struggle
        • Lebensraum – Hitler’s right of superior nations to “living space”
