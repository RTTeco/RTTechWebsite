Development of the Cold War
 Section 1 – Big Idea
  •  A period of conflict known as the Cold War developed between the United States & the Soviet Union after 1945, dividing Europe.
3
Essential Questions
  • What differences between the Soviet Union & United States led to the conflict known as the Cold War?


  • List & explain 2 conflicts that took place during the Cold War?
4
Confrontation of the Superpowers
  • After the war the difference between the US & USSR were clear and come into conflict
    • US feared Communism
    • USSR feared capitalism
    • Neither side would give up any territory in the East or West

  • Superpower – country with both military powers & political influence
5
Rivalry in Europe
  • Eastern Europe


    • US & Great Britain believed the liberated nations should hold free elections to determine their futures


    • Stalin worried the nations would choose capitalism, so the Soviet army stayed in those nations after the war
6
The Truman Doctrine
  • Truman was concerned over the possible spread of Communism into Greece and other Mediterranean areas
    • 1946 – Britain stopped giving aid to Greece to fight Communism
  • March 1947 – Truman asked congress for $400 million in aid for Greece & Turkey
  • Truman Doctrine – US promise & policy of anti-communism support
  • If the Soviet expansion was not stopped we would face the spread of Communism all over the world
7
Marshall Plan
  • Following the Truman Doctrine was another plan to aid Europe


  • Marshall Plan – US effort to rebuild war torn Europe
    • Sent $13 billion to help with economic problems
    • Soviet viewed as the US buying off the political futures of nations


  • Soviets responded with their own plan but failed because they couldn’t provide the money for aid


  • By 1947 – US & USSR had a split in Europe


  • Policy of Containment – to keep Communism within its existing boundaries and prevent further Soviet aggressive moves.
8
Division of Germany
  • Fate of Germany was also a source of conflict


  • Allies divided Germany into 4 parts
    • United States
    • Great Britain
    • France
    • Soviet Union


  • Berlin located deep inside the Soviets zone was also divided into 4 parts
  • 1948 – US, GB & France were making plans to unified the 3 parts to make a West German democratic nation
  • Soviets opposed this idea
9
Berlin Airlift
  • Soviets started a blockade of West Berlin
    • Soviet troops allowed neither trucks, trains nor barges to enter the west part of the city of Berlin
    • 2.5 million people were cut off from food & supplies


  • Western powers did not want another war, but did not want to give in to Communism pressure


  • Berlin Air Lift – tactic used to overcome a Soviet blockade
    • US & GB planes would drop supplies
    • Lasted 10 months - 200,000 flights - 2.3 million tons

11
Germany Divided

  • Soviets gave up the blockade in May 1949


  • Sept. 1949 – Federal Republic of Germany was formed by the aid of the US, GB, France
    • Known as West Germany, free democratic nation


  • Dec. 1949 – German Democratic Republic was formed by the Soviets
    • Known as East Germany, Soviet controlled communist nation
12
Arms Race
  • 1949
    • Chiang Kai-shek loses control in China
    • Mao Zedong sets up the communist People’s Republic of China
    • Soviet Union explodes its 1st Atomic bomb

  • All these events strengthens the US fear of the spread of Communism


  • Arms Race – build up of armies and weapons
    • US & USSR raced to see who could build a deadlier bomb (Nuclear)
    • Both believed a large arsenal of nuclear weapons would prevent war, because if one attacked the other they would still have time to respond and attack back
13
New Military Alliances
  • 1945-1949 a series of conflicts lead the 2 superpowers to form alliances


  • NATO – North Atlantic Treaty Organization, an alliance formed in 1949 by the US and its European Allies
    • Belgium, Luxemborg, France, Netherland, GB, Italy, Denmark, Norway, Portugal, Canada, West Germany, Turkey, Greece  & Iceland
    • To provide mutual help if any one was attacked
  • Warsaw Pact – military alliance of the USSR & its Soviet allies formed in 1955
    • Albania, Bulgaria, Czechoslovakia, East Germany, Hungary, Poland &  Romania


  • Europe was once again divided into a hostile alliance system just like before WW I

Korean War
  • 1950 – Korean War
    • Sparked more American fear of Communist expansion
    • Communist gov’t of North Korea supported by the USSR tried to take over democratic South Korea
    • US sent troops to help fight the war


  • US wanted to contain Communism so they set up more alliances

More Military Alliances
  • Southeast Asia Treaty Organization (SEATO)
    • US, GB, France, Pakistan, Thailand, Philippines, Australia, New Zealand
  • Central Treaty Organization (CENTO)
    • US, GB, Turkey, Iraq, Iran, Pakistan
  • By mid 1950’s – US had military alliances with 42 nations
  • Weapons build up by US & USSR
    • More nuclear weapons
    • Hydrogen bomb
    • Intercontinental ballistic missiles (ICBM)
  • Deterrence – policy that huge arsenals of nuclear weapons on both sides would prevent war

Berlin Wall
  • Nikita Khrushchev – leader who replaced Stalin after his death in 1955
  • West Berlin was an island of prosperity in the middle of Communist territory
  • Many people tried to escape East Germany & Communist repression thru West Berlin
  • 1961 – Communists build a wall separating East Berlin from West Berlin
  • Massive barrier with barb wire, floodlights, machine-gun towers, minefields and vicious dog patrols
  • Became a striking symbol of division between the two superpowers

Cuban Missile Crisis
  • 1959 – Cold War reached frightening levels
    • John F. Kennedy – US
    • Nikita Khrushchev – USSR
    • Cuban Fidel Castro, supported by the USSR overthrew the Cuban gov’t
    • A Communist nation within 90 miles of the US was a threat
    • US could not attack Cuba without threat of a Soviet retaliation, causing a nuclear war
    • 1961 - Kennedy & CIA helped exiled Cuban fighters invade Cuba at the Bay of Pigs, hope to cause a revolt against Castro.
    • Attempt failed and lasted only 3 days
20
Cuban Missile Crisis
  • 1962
    • USSR sent Communist advisors to Cuba
    • USSR then began to send missiles to Cuba to counter the missiles the US placed in Turkey.
    • US would not allow nuclear weapons so close to the US
    • Kennedy placed a blockade of US ships outside to Cuba to stop the Soviet ships
    • Soviets stopped & turned around only if Kennedy agreed not to invade Cuba again
  • Closest point we came to a nuclear war


  • 1963 – a direct hotline communication system was installed between Moscow & Washington DC

    • Despite massive superiority by American forces we failed to beat the North Vietnamese

    • South Vietnam fell to communism
    • 1975 – Laos & Cambodia fell to communism

Soviet Union
  • WWII devastated the USSR, expected workers to produce goods with little return for themselves


  • Heavy Industry – manufacture of machines & equipment for factories & mines


  • Stalin was the master of communism & the Soviet Union
    • Stalin died on March 5, 1955

Khrushchev Era
  • He took steps to change the USSR


  • De-Stalinization – Khrushchev’s policy of undoing Stalin’s more ruthless policies
    • Loosened gov’t control
    • Placed more emphasis on consumer goods
    • Made bad decisions with agriculture & foreign policy


  • 1964 – while on vacation the Soviet leaders voted him out of office because of  “deteriorating health”

Eastern Europe
  • 1945-1947 – Soviet-controlled Communist gov’ts became firmly entrenched in:
    • East Germany, Bulgaria, Romania, Poland, Hungary, Czechoslovakia
    • Followed Stalin’s examples of Communist rule

Revolts against Communism
  • Communism developed bad feeling by the peoples of Eastern Europe
    • USSR exploited the peoples, land & made living conditions harsh


  • Even after Stalin’s death, the USSR made it clear it would not let Eastern European nations become independent

Totalitarian Gov’t & Command Economy
  • No protection of human rights or civil liberties


  • Govt controls all property & means of production


  • Govt makes all economic decisions


  • Govt controls supply & demand

Democratic Gov’t & Market Economy
  • Private ownership of property & production


  • Private individuals and businesses make economic decisions with limited govt interference


  • Supply & demand dictates production

The Brezhnev Era
  • Leonid Brezhnev – replaced Khrushchev
  •     in 1964 as the leader of the USSR
    • Brezhnev Doctrine – right of the Soviet Union to intervene if communism was threatened in another nation
    • Détente – pre-1979 period of relaxed US – USSR relations
    • Brezhnev allowed more access to Western styles of music, dress & art
    • Dissidents – people who speak out against a regime


  • 1970’s – Communist ruling class (army leaders, KGB, Party leaders) became corrupt & enjoyed a high standard of living

Cold War Intensifies
  • 1970’s – détente allowed US grain and
  •                    goods to be sold to the USSR


  • 1979 – Soviet Union invaded Afghanistan
    • Began  a new period of East-West confrontations
    • USSR wanted a Communist gov’t in Afghanistan


  • US president Jimmy Carter showed our disapproval by:
    • Canceling US participation in the 1980 Olympics in Moscow
    • Placed an embargo on grain
39
US Response to Afghanistan
  • President Ronald Reagan:
    • Called the Soviet Union the “EVIL EMPIRE”
    • Began a new era of military buildup & arms race
    • Gave military aid to the Afghan rebels
    • 1984 Olympics in Los Angeles – USSR boycotts the games
40
Gorbachev & Reform
  • By the 1980’s the Soviet Union had:
    • A declining economy
    • A rising infant mortality rate
    • Poor working conditions


  • Mikhail Gorbachev – became Communist Party leader in 1985, lead a new era in the USSR
    • Perestroika – Gorbachev’s initial economic reforms or restructuring
    • Realized economic reform would not work without political reform
    • 1988 – set up a new political system with a Soviet parliament
    • 1990 Gorbachev became the 1st & last president of the USSR
41
End of Cold War
  • Gorbachev’s “New Thinking” of Soviet foreign policy led to stunning changes in the Soviet Union
    • Suddenly ended the Cold War – process took from 1987-1991
    • 1987 – INF Treaty – agreement to eliminate mid-range nuclear weapons
      • USSR wanted to concentrate on social & economic reform
      • US wanted to reduce military spending & the stop national debt
    • USSR quit giving military support to other Communist nations
      • Opened the door to overthrow the current Communist gov’ts
      • Most revolts were peaceful & swept thru Eastern Europe
    • 1990 – Germany was reunified, biggest symbol of the Cold War
    • 1991 – USSR was dissolved & Russian Republic was formed
    • Long rivalry between the 2 superpowers was over

  • 1989-1990 – Independence of European Nations

The New Russia
  • Boris Yeltsin – Dec 25, 1991 took over as the 1st president of Russia after Gorbachev resigned
    • Yeltsin wanted a free market economy
    • Transition was hard, social disarray, rise in organized crime
  • Vladimir Putin – 1999 took over for Yeltsin who resigned, won the election in 2000 was a former KGB officer
    • 2001 – introduced reforms to boost growth & income, tax cuts & land sales
    • Russia experienced income surplus & a growing economy in energy & other markets
    • Also have new social problems similar to the US
